# The 2025 Breakouts

*The 2025 NFL season produced a cleaner-than-usual set of dynasty breakouts — several players who had been waiting behind established starters finally got their shots and seized them. A handful of rookies accelerated their development timelines. Two veterans reclaimed roles the market had written off.*

## Quarterback

### Shedeur Sanders, Cleveland Browns — SPECULATIVE BUY-LOW

Sanders was drafted in the 5th round and entered 2025 as a backup — never establishing himself as Cleveland's starter. His 2025 stat line: ~1,400 yards, 7 TDs, 10 INTs in 8 games with 1 completion in limited action as a backup. The accuracy profile is genuine; the opportunity was not.

His accuracy profile is elite for the position, and his release is among the quickest in the class. In Superflex formats, his name carries value. But 2025 was not a breakout — it was a development year in obscurity. The dynasty buy thesis requires Cleveland giving him a genuine starting shot in 2026 or a trade to a better situation.

**Age 23 entering 2026.** This is a buy-low on ceiling, not a confirmed breakout. Acquire cheaply; wait for opportunity.

## Running Back

### Omarion Hampton, Los Angeles Chargers — BUY

Hampton was the most complete RB prospect in the 2025 class and the Chargers gave him the lead role early. He showed the profile in limited time — 545 rushing yards, 4 TDs, and 32 receptions in 9 games before a hamstring injury sent him to IR. Per-game production was encouraging; the season was cut short before the full volume materialized.

Jim Harbaugh's offense uses the back heavily in screens and check-downs, and Hampton showed genuine pass protection aptitude before the injury. Age 22, in a run-heavy offense — the 2026 season is the proving ground.

**The buy thesis remains intact — the injury clouds it.** Buy at the post-IR discount before training camp reports confirm his health.

### Ashton Jeanty, Las Vegas Raiders — BUY (Aggressive)

Jeanty entered 2025 as perhaps the most college-decorated RB prospect in years (2,600+ yards at Boise State) and delivered a solid first season: **975 rushing yards, 9 total TDs (5 rush + 4 receiving), 3.7 yards per carry** — the Raiders' weak offensive line suppressed his efficiency numbers below college expectations, but his touchdown production was genuine.

The concern heading into the draft was Mountain West competition. His efficiency in the NFL was lower than projected because of system/OL issues, not because of talent. The passing game usage is a positive development (49 receptions for 266 yards). Age 22, on a team rebuilding around him.

**Buy aggressively if available.** Year 2 in a better situation or with OL upgrades could unlock elite-tier efficiency.

## Wide Receiver

### Tetairoa McMillan, Carolina Panthers — BUY

McMillan was the consensus WR1 in the 2025 class and the Panthers gave him immediate volume despite being a rebuilding team. He finished with **70 receptions, 1,014 yards, and 7 touchdowns** — winning the Offensive Rookie of the Year Award and setting a Panthers franchise rookie receiving record.

His size-speed combination (6'4", 4.45) is generational. Dynasty skepticism about the Panthers' offense was initially warranted but has now been resolved — even on a below-average offense, he produced WR2-caliber dynasty numbers. When the Panthers' QB situation stabilizes, he becomes a true WR1.

**Age 22. Must own. Pay the premium.**

### Elic Ayomanor, Tennessee Titans — HOLD / BUY-LOW

Ayomanor was a 4th-round pick in a crowded Titans offense. His 2025 line: **41 receptions, 515 yards, 4 touchdowns** — rotational production rather than a featured role. His route running is genuinely advanced for a first-year player, but the volume was limited by the Titans' depth chart.

The **HOLD/BUY-LOW** signal remains appropriate: the talent and age (22) are real, but 2025 was not a breakout. It was a development year. The Titans' offense and his role within it need to expand before he warrants buy investment.

---

## Tight End

### Colston Loveland, Chicago Bears — BUY

Loveland was the crown jewel of the 2025 TE class — a player whose athleticism drew comparisons to Kyle Pitts — and he rewarded the teams that took him early. Chicago gave him an immediate role. He finished with **58 receptions, 713 yards, and 6 touchdowns** — a strong rookie debut building real chemistry with Caleb Williams.

The Bears' young offense, centered on Caleb Williams, is only going to grow. Loveland and Williams are building chemistry in real-time. Age 22, in a high-volume passing offense.

**This is a must-own dynasty asset. If you do not own him, pay the premium to acquire him.**

---

## The Common Thread

Five of six breakouts are **age 22**. This is not coincidence — it is the dynasty sweet spot. Players who break out at 22 have the highest probability of sustaining that production across 4-6 more NFL seasons. The market hasn't fully priced in the longevity premium on young breakouts. That asymmetry is the opportunity.

# 2026 NFL Draft: Running Back Class Guide

*One generational prospect. A meaningful cliff. This is a top-heavy class — manage your dynasty expectations accordingly.*

> ⚠️ **Pre-draft notice**: Written before the 2026 NFL Draft (April 24-26). RB landing spots are the single biggest value determinant. Revise after draft night.

---

## Class Verdict

**Top-heavy. Jeremiyah Love is the clear 1.01; everyone else carries a starting-caliber ceiling but is not a dynasty cornerstone.** The depth at RB in 2026 does not match the WR tier — do not reach for RBs 3-6 at WR2 prices in your rookie draft.

---

## Tier 1 — Dynasty RB1

### Jeremiyah Love — Notre Dame | 6'0", 212 lbs

**The generational prospect.** 1,372 rushing yards, 18 rushing TDs, 6.9 YPC in 2025. Won the 2025 Doak Walker Award. Heisman Trophy finalist. Set Notre Dame's single-season record with 21 total TDs in 2025. One of only six FBS players in history with multiple seasons averaging 6.5+ YPC and 15+ rushing TDs.

At the 2026 NFL Combine, he ran an official **4.36-second 40-yard dash** — 4th-best by any RB at 6'0"+, 210+ lbs since 2003. The Saquon Barkley comparison is not idle flattery; multiple team sources have used it explicitly.

His receiving ability is often underestimated. He had 27 catches in Notre Dame's run-heavy scheme — his NFL receiving usage will be significantly higher. His pass protection grade was already strong, meaning he won't be subbed out on passing downs.

**Dynasty Ceiling/Floor**: Ceiling is an RB1 dynasty cornerstone for years. Floor is still an RB1 — the athleticism and contact balance are NFL-ready even with a modest landing spot.

**NFL Draft Projection**: Top 10, multiple mocks project picks 3–8. Some evaluators grade him as the best player at any position in the class.

**Dynasty Target**: 1.01. No debate.

---

## Tier 2 — Day 2 Targets with Starting Upside

### Emmett Johnson — Nebraska | ~5'11", ~205 lbs

**The PPR value play.** 1,451 rushing yards (4th nationally) in 2025, plus **46 receptions — 1st among all RBs nationally**. 297 total touches. Forced 68 missed tackles (1st among RBs per PFF). His contact balance and change-of-direction quickness give him a real floor even in a committee.

The caveat: yards after contact per attempt was below average among RBs — he's not a bruiser. He needs a scheme that values receiving backs (KC, NE, SF types would elevate him). The limited red-zone value is the ceiling constraint.

**Dynasty Ceiling/Floor**: Ceiling is a PPR-relevant RB2 who contributes across all three downs. "Poor man's Shady McCoy" comp has circulated. Floor is a change-of-pace back who never secures the lead role.

**In Superflex PPR**: This is the best remaining value in the class after Love. 46 receptions at the NFL level translates directly to PPR points. Buy at the late-first/early-second price.

**NFL Draft Projection**: Round 2-3.

**Dynasty Target**: Late 1st or early 2nd round.

---

### Nicholas Singleton — Penn State | 6'0", 219 lbs

**The injury gamble.** Broke Saquon Barkley's Penn State record for career rushing and all-purpose touchdowns. Strong hands out of the backfield, kick return ability. Did not participate at the 2026 NFL Combine due to a foot injury — this is the central risk.

Pre-injury, Singleton was a potential top-RB prospect with inside and outside zone ability. The foot injury changes his draft range and dynasty floor until cleared.

**Dynasty Ceiling/Floor**: Ceiling (if healthy) is an early-down starter with pass-catching ability. Floor with uncertainty is significant downgrade — could land as Day 3 pick in a committee role.

**Dynasty Target**: Conditional 2nd-round pick. **Buy at the injury discount if the foot is cleared; fade if uncertainty persists through draft week.**

---

### Kaytron Allen — Penn State | 5'11", 217 lbs

1,303 rushing yards, 15 TDs, 6.2 YPC in 2025. Physical downhill runner, excels in short-yardage. But only **18 receptions** in 2025 — the dynasty red flag in PPR formats. NFL teams increasingly want three-down backs; Allen's profile skews two-down.

**Dynasty Target**: Late 2nd/early 3rd round. **Standard-scoring leagues only.** Fade in PPR.

---

### Mike Washington Jr. — Arkansas | 6'2", 223 lbs

1,070 yards, 8 TDs, 6.4 YPC. Regarded as the best pure power back in the class. Very limited receiving usage. His profile is a two-down grinder — he will never be a PPR asset.

**Dynasty Target**: 3rd round. **Standard-scoring leagues only.** Fade in PPR.

---

### Jonah Coleman — Washington | 5'8", 220 lbs

Compact bowling ball with exceptional tackle-breaking ability per PFF. Strong vision between the tackles, finds creases before they open. Limited size limits receiving projection.

**Dynasty Target**: 3rd round/waiver-wire add. High floor in a volume role; low ceiling in PPR leagues.

---

## BMFFFL Implications

In a **Superflex PPR** format, the RB calculus is clear:

- **Love at 1.01** is a no-debate pick — generational talent, Barkley comp, three-down projection
- **Emmett Johnson** is the best PPR value in the class given his 46-reception profile; target in the late 1st or early 2nd
- **Allen and Washington** are effectively undraftable in PPR dynasty until their roles prove receiving involvement
- **Grandes** (picking at 1.01 and ~1.05) will take Love and likely Hampton/Johnson at their second pick — they're the primary RB beneficiary in this draft
- Standard RB positional depth note: after Love and Johnson, the 2026 RB class does not justify high second-round cost; pivot to the deep WR pool

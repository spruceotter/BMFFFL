# 2026 NFL Draft: Tight End Class Guide

*One elite prospect. Solid depth behind him. The standard TE dynasty caution applies: even the best prospects in this class require patience.*

> ⚠️ **Pre-draft notice**: Written before the 2026 NFL Draft (April 24-26). TE value is highly sensitive to landing spot and target environment.

---

## Class Verdict

**One first-round dynasty prospect (Sadiq), followed by a solid-but-projection-heavy second tier.** Eli Stowers offers the most unique athlete in the class. Klare, Trigg, and Royer are developmental stashes. Do not expect immediate production from anyone outside of Sadiq — and even he carries the inherent TE timeline risk.

---

## Tier 1 — First-Round Dynasty Pick

### Kenyon Sadiq — Oregon | 6'3"–6'4", 241 lbs

**The generational athlete.** At the 2026 NFL Combine, Sadiq ran an official **4.39-second 40-yard dash** — the fastest by a tight end at the NFL Combine since at least 2003, breaking Vernon Davis's prior record of 4.40. Vertical jump: 43.5 inches. Broad jump: 11'1". These are not just "good for a TE" numbers — they are elite absolute athlete numbers at any position.

His college production: 80 career receptions, 892 yards, 11 TDs. In 2025: 51 receptions, 560 yards, 8 TDs — led all college TEs in touchdown receptions. His 2024 efficiency was exceptional: 25-of-27 targets (93%), 80.4 PFF receiving grade, 10.1 YAC per catch.

He's a receiver-first TE deployed primarily in detached alignments — slot, out wide, H-back. He is already a willing and functional blocker in space (ahead of where most receiving TEs are at this stage). He will not be asked to anchor in-line as a primary job early in his career.

**The one caveat**: his career 1.72 yards per route run was the lowest of the top-5 TEs in the class — partly explained by Oregon's loaded receiving room and competition from fellow TE Terrance Ferguson. Worth noting as a production-based caveat on his projection, but the athleticism numbers override it.

**Dynasty Ceiling/Floor**: Ceiling is a bona fide TE1 — the recent comp range is Sam LaPorta to Brock Bowers to Tyler Warren. Floor is a contributing TE2 who earns passing-down snaps but never claims a full-season target load.

**Timeline**: Sadiq is the one player in this class with a realistic Year 1 contribution path, given his athleticism and receiver-first deployment. Expect a developmental season with spot-start upside, followed by a **Year 2 breakout window** — consistent with the LaPorta/Bowers template.

**NFL Draft Projection**: Round 1, picks 13–20 overall. The only TE projected to go in the first round.

**Dynasty Target**: 1st-round dynasty rookie pick in TE-premium leagues. High-end 2nd-round pick in standard formats (where TE is historically undervalued early).

## Tier 2 — Developmental Upside

### Eli Stowers — Vanderbilt | ~6'5", 235 lbs

**The sleeper.** Former quarterback. At the 2026 NFL Combine, Stowers posted a **45.5-inch vertical jump — a tight end combine record**, and the second-highest vertical jump recorded at the Combine regardless of position since 2006 (all-position record: 46 inches, Gerald Sensabaugh, 2005). Also ran a 4.51 forty and recorded an 11'3" broad jump.

His college production: 62 receptions, 769 yards, 4 TDs in 2025 with an 85.3 PFF receiving grade and 2.55 yards per route run — best in the class. Career 2.34 Y/RR and 1.51 yards per team pass attempt, both class-leading. Forced 21 missed tackles on 112 career receptions (19% missed tackle rate). Lined up primarily in the slot and detached alignments.

The former QB background is a real wildcard — his feel for coverage leverage, zone manipulation, and spacing compresses the typical TE learning curve meaningfully.

**Dynasty Ceiling/Floor**: Ceiling is a TE2 who earns 60+ targets per season — a matchup problem in 12-personnel as a big slot who exploits linebackers. Floor is a complementary TE who cycles through rosters.

**Timeline**: Year 2–3 for meaningful dynasty production.

**Dynasty Target**: High-end 2nd-round pick in TE-premium leagues. 3rd-round stash in standard formats.

---

### Max Klare — Ohio State (formerly Purdue) | ~6'5", 250 lbs

Receiver-first profile, fluid movement for the position. Productive across two schools with a high PFF receiving grade and significant target volume. Blocking has been inconsistent — this is the clearest weakness. Ceiling is a receiving TE2 with 50-catch upside in the right offense.

**Dynasty Target**: Mid-2nd-round in TE-premium formats. Late-round stash otherwise. Timeline: Year 2–3.

---

### Michael Trigg — Baylor | 6'4", 240 lbs

1.87 career Y/RR, rising to 2.18 in 2025. Aligned in the slot 66.9% of the time in college. Exceptional body control and timing on above/below-frame throws. Ceiling is a developmental TE2; timeline is 2-3 seasons.

**Dynasty Target**: 3rd-round pick. Patient stash in deep leagues.

---

### Joe Royer — Cincinnati | ~6'5", ~245 lbs

6th-year senior — older prospect with compressed development and compressed ceiling. Reliable hands, solid catch-point skills. Floor is a career TE2/complementary piece.

**Dynasty Target**: Deep league stash only.

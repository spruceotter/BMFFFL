# Owners Meeting 2026: What's on the Agenda

*The BMFFFL Owners Meeting happens every May. It's part league business, part rule review, and part argument about scoring settings that will ultimately change nothing. Here's what usually happens and what might actually change in 2026.*

---

## What an Owners Meeting Is

The BMFFFL Owners Meeting is the annual league governance event. All 12 owners (or their representatives) gather — typically via video call — to conduct league business before the season's primary events:

1. **Rule change proposals** — any owner can propose; majority vote decides
2. **Rookie draft order confirmation** — officially locked based on 2025 final standings
3. **Waiver system review** — FAAB budget resets and any adjustments
4. **Playoff structure confirmation** — seeds, bracket, tiebreakers
5. **Any contested transactions** from the previous season get final resolution

**2026 Owners Meeting timing**: May 2026 (exact date TBD by commissioner). Typically 2-3 weeks before the rookie draft to allow owners time to adjust strategy based on any rule changes.

## Potential Rule Change Proposals for 2026

These are the agenda items most likely to generate discussion:

### 1. TE Premium Scoring
**What it is**: Assign a bonus to TE receptions (e.g., +0.25 or +0.5 per reception for TEs, vs. standard 1.0 for all).
**Why it might come up**: Dynasty leagues increasingly use TE premium scoring to differentiate elite TEs from average ones. Given the league has Colston Loveland on Cogdeill11 and Bijan Robinson at RB as clear tier separations, TE premium would dramatically reshape dynasty values.
**Who benefits**: Teams with elite TEs (Cogdeill11 with Loveland, Tubes94 with Kyle Pitts if he finally delivers). Rebuilding teams get a signal on which TEs to draft.
**Likelihood of passing**: Medium. TE premium is growing in popularity, but changes to scoring always generate dissent from teams without top TEs.

### 2. Taxi Squad Expansion
**What it is**: Expand the taxi squad from 5 spots to 7.
**Why it might come up**: Dynasty leagues often expand taxi squads to help teams develop more rookies without roster cuts.
**Who benefits**: Rebuilding teams (Grandes, Escuelas) who need to develop multiple 2026 picks without immediately losing them to waivers.
**Likelihood of passing**: High. Low-controversy change that helps rebuild teams without hurting contenders meaningfully.

### 3. Superflex QB Dynasty Value Adjustment
**What it is**: Changes to passing TD point values (currently 4 pts) or first-down bonuses.
**Why it might come up**: Superflex leagues use QBs heavily. With Lamar Jackson, Caleb Williams, Trevor Lawrence all in dynasty prime, the QB slot is contested.
**Likelihood of passing**: Low. Scoring format changes are the most contested and least likely to get majority votes.

### 4. Rookie Draft Round Count
**What it is**: Expand from 4 rounds to 5 rounds for the 2026 draft specifically, given the WR depth of the class.
**Why it might come up**: A 5-round rookie draft would allow more picks to be used on depth WRs from the 2026 class.
**Who benefits**: Teams with more picks (Grandes has 10 in 2026 — they'd love a 5th round to add even more WR depth).
**Likelihood of passing**: Medium. Adds time to the draft event; contending teams may not want to spend the extra time.

## What to Actually Expect

Owners Meetings in most leagues follow a pattern:
- 80% discussion, 20% formal decisions
- One or two rule changes pass; the rest are deferred to "next year"
- The draft order is confirmed, FAAB resets are agreed on
- Someone complains about the waiver wire system

The most consequential item for 2026 is almost certainly the **TE premium scoring** proposal if it comes up, as it would reshape dynasty values more than any other potential change. The **taxi squad expansion** is likely the easiest vote.

## After the Meeting

Once the Owners Meeting concludes (typically 1-2 weeks before the rookie draft), the strategic implications become concrete:
- Final draft order locked → pick values can be accurately modeled
- Any rule changes → dynasty values shift (especially TEs if premium passes)
- FAAB budgets reset → all 12 teams start the season with equal bid capital

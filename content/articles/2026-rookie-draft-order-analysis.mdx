## The Linear Format: Why Order Matters More Than You Think

In a linear dynasty rookie draft, the pick order is replicated in every round. If you pick 1.01, you also pick 2.01, 3.01, and 4.01. This amplifies the value difference between positions.

**1.01 vs. 1.12**: The gap in player quality is significant. But the gap compounds across rounds — the owner at 1.01 gets the best player in all four rounds, while 1.12 gets the worst.

This is why **late finishes matter enormously in dynasty**. A Moodie Bowl season doesn't just get you a top pick — it gets you four top picks.

## Strategic Implications by Position in the Order

### Picking 1.01-1.03: Play it straight
You have a clear top player at your selection. Draft him. Don't trade away this pick for veteran value — the pick compounds in rounds 2-4 where you're also picking at the top.

### Picking 1.04-1.08: Identify your tier break
You're picking after the consensus top tier. Know in advance: is there a clear 2nd-tier player you want, or is the board relatively even? This determines whether to "reach" for positional need or take best-available.

### Picking 1.09-1.12: The trade-up window
If there's a specific player you need and you're picking late, round 1 is the time to trade up. Offering a future pick to a rebuilding team for an earlier selection is reasonable — you're paying with future assets for current certainty.

### Round 2 Strategy
In linear drafts, round 2 often has the second-best player at each position. The owner at 2.01 gets the best remaining player after 12 round-1 selections. This can be genuinely excellent value:
- WR3 of the class who dropped due to landing spot concerns
- TE2 (Eli Stowers if he slips past round 1)
- A developmental QB who didn't get the round-1 hype

### Rounds 3-4: Volume and Taxi
Late round picks are taxi squad territory. Take the highest-ceiling, lowest-floor players available and taxi them for 1-2 years. 3rd and 4th round picks rarely contribute immediately in dynasty — don't force it.

## What Trades to Expect Before the Draft

The month of May is peak trade season for dynasty picks. Expect:

1. **Teams with multiple early picks** (Escuelas, Cmaleski) to package some for veterans
2. **Win-now teams** (MLSchools12, Tubes94) to offer future picks for moving up to specific prospects
3. **Teams picking 1.05-1.08** to be the most active in offers — they're in the zone where a small trade-up could secure a specific player

The deadline for trades in the BMFFFL is active year-round (outside the Week 14 regular-season deadline). Pick trades are valid any time.

## 2020: Cogdeill11 — The Founding Champion

The inaugural Sleeper season ended in a four-point thriller. Cogdeill11 (10-3, 2nd seed) beat eldridsm 203-198 in a championship where both teams scored above their regular-season averages. The hero was Alvin Kamara, who scored 6 rushing touchdowns on Christmas Day — the most in NFL history — and carried the roster across the finish line at exactly the right moment.

The more significant result: MLSchools12, at 11-2 with 2,115 points, lost to eldridsm in the semis. The most dominant regular-season team in the league was eliminated in the first round of the playoffs. It was the opening act of a story that would repeat itself.

**Where are they now**: Cogdeill11 has not returned to the championship since. 2021: 9-5, round-1 exit by 3.62 points. 2022-2025: .400 win rate, no playoff appearances. The dynasty window closed fast. This is typical.

## 2021: MLSchools12 — The Ring That Explains Everything

After being robbed in 2020, MLSchools12 came back with a vengeance. 11-3, #1 seed, dominant throughout. The 150-103 championship win over rbr was the least dramatic championship in BMFFFL history by margin — the only time the top seed cruised to the title without drama. Lamar Jackson and Kyler Murray as the SF QB pair was an overwhelming advantage in a league where superflex scoring multiplies elite QB value.

This is the ring that explains why we still watch MLSchools12: they can do it. They have. They're the only owner to win multiple championships, and they did it from the #1 seed while running a historically dominant regular season. The 2021 season is their proof of concept.

**What it means**: The #1 seed curse is real — MLSchools12 is 1-3 in the semis as a top seed. 2021 is the exception. When they're right, they're unstoppable.

## 2022: Grandes — The Hot-Take Proof

Grandes went 4-9 in 2020. Then 10-4 in 2021 (playoff exit round 1). Then 8-6 in 2022 — barely made the playoffs as the 4th seed — and won the championship. The arc is the purest version of what dynasty rewards: patient roster building, a 2-3 year accumulation phase, then a peak-window run when the pieces mature simultaneously.

The championship game was close: 137-115 over rbr (who was making their second straight championship appearance). rbr became the first two-time runner-up in BMFFFL history this year.

**Where they are now**: The Grandes arc closed hard. 2023: 9-5, semifinal exit. 2024: 7-7. 2025: 4-10, Moodie Bowl (last place). Three years from champion to last. The dynasty window is real — and it closes.

---

## 2023: JuicyBussy — The Last Seed

The most improbable championship in BMFFFL history. JuicyBussy entered the playoffs as the 6th and final seed with an 8-6 record. They won three straight games to claim the title. The championship win over eldridm20 — 179-149 — wasn't close on the day, but getting there required beating every team seeded above them.

The 2023 champion was also the 2024 regular-season scoring leader: 2,036.54 points, which was the second-highest total in the Sleeper era. JuicyBussy was genuinely elite by this point. They just needed a path.

**The lesson**: In a 6-team playoff format, the last seed has won the championship. Getting in is the hard part.

---

## 2024: MLSchools12 — Ring Number Two

After three years of near-misses (2022 semis by 2.80 pts, 2023 semis as the 13-1 team), MLSchools12 won their second championship as the 3rd seed. The win over SexMachineAndyD — 168-146 — was workmanlike, not dominant. What changed from the failure years? Seed pressure. As the #3 seed, there were no expectations, no "best team should win" narrative. They just played.

This is the interesting MLSchools12 paradox: they win championships as a lower seed (2021 #1 was the exception), and they lose them when they're supposed to win. The 2024 ring may be the cleanest evidence of that.

**Two-time champion. The only one.** MLSchools12 (2021, 2024) is in a tier alone. Nobody else has repeated.

---

## 2025: tdtd19844 — The Long Arc

If the Grandes arc (4-9 to champion in three years) was the first sign that dynasty windows work, tdtd19844's 2025 title confirmed it as a structural truth of the league. 2022: 3-11. 2023-2024: gradual improvement. 2025: 8-6, 4th seed, championship.

The final was 152-135 over Tubes94 (who was making their first championship appearance). Not a blowout, but convincing enough.

**The defending champion outlook**: History is not kind to BMFFFL title holders. No one has ever won back-to-back. MLSchools12 went 13-1 the year after their 2024 championship — and lost in the semis. tdtd19844 enters 2026 as the defending champion with a target on their back and a roster that peaked last season.

## Six Seasons, Six Patterns

### 1. The #1 seed curse
MLSchools12's record from the top seed: 1-3. The 13-1 record has happened twice. Both times, they lost in the semis to an 8-6 team.

### 2. Eight-and-six is the championship formula
Three of six champions finished 8-6. If you make the playoffs, you have a shot. Regular season record is not predictive of championship outcomes.

### 3. Dynasty windows close fast
Every champion — Cogdeill11, Grandes, JuicyBussy, tdtd19844 — followed a build-peak-collapse arc. Average peak: 2-3 seasons. The Grandes example is the extreme: champion in 2022, last place in 2025.

### 4. No back-to-back champions
Six seasons, five different champions. MLSchools12 is the only multiple champion. The field catches up; defending champions regress.

### 5. The scoring leader almost never wins
The regular-season scoring leader has won the championship once (MLSchools12, 2021). In four other seasons, the highest-scoring team was eliminated before the final.

### 6. Superflex QBs are the title variable
In every championship, the winner had elite QB usage in a superflex format. Two elite QBs > one. This is the single biggest roster construction lever in the league.

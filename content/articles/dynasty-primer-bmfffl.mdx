## Understanding Dynasty Fantasy: A BMFFFL Primer

Dynasty fantasy football is different from redraft leagues. In redraft, you build a new roster every year. In dynasty, you keep your players year after year — building, trading, and developing talent over multiple seasons.

The BMFFFL has been running since 2020. If you're new to the league or new to dynasty in general, this guide covers everything you need to compete.

---

## The Core Difference: Ownership vs. Rental

In a redraft league, you draft a player in August and they're gone the following March. In dynasty, **you own that player as long as he's active in the NFL** (and you choose to keep him).

This changes everything about how you value players:
- A 22-year-old RB with upside is worth more than a 28-year-old RB with current production
- A rookie QB you can develop for 8 seasons is more valuable than a veteran you rent for 1
- Draft capital (future picks) has real value because you can use it to acquire young players

The BMFFFL has been running six years. That means the players drafted in the 2020 startup draft have been on rosters for six seasons. **Christian McCaffrey** — drafted by MLSchools12 in that startup — has been a cornerstone of one of the most dominant rosters in BMFFFL history.

## BMFFFL Format Details

Understanding the league settings is the foundation of good roster construction.

### Roster Slots
- **1 QB** — starting quarterback
- **2 RB** — two running backs
- **2 WR** — two wide receivers
- **1 TE** — tight end
- **1 FLEX** — RB/WR/TE
- **1 SF (Superflex)** — QB/RB/WR/TE (almost always a second QB)
- **6 BN** — bench players
- **1 IR** — injured reserve
- **3 TAXI** — taxi squad (explained below)

**Total active roster**: 15 players + 3 taxi = 18 total.

### Scoring
The BMFFFL is **Full PPR** (1.0 points per reception). Every catch counts. This makes pass-catching backs and high-volume slot receivers more valuable.

Key scoring settings:
- **4 points per passing TD** (not 6)
- **1 point per reception** (full PPR)
- Standard yardage scoring (1 pt/10 rushing/receiving, 1 pt/25 passing)

The 4-point passing TD is significant: it slightly deflates QB value compared to 6-point TD leagues, but Superflex more than compensates for this.

### Superflex Is the Most Important Setting

The SF slot means you start **two quarterbacks**. This is the defining format of the BMFFFL.

Implication: QB is the scarcest position in dynasty. If you're weak at QB, you're fighting with one arm tied behind your back. The teams that have consistently won in BMFFFL (MLSchools12, JuicyBussy) have had top-3 QB rooms.

Don't undervalue rookie QBs. A 22-year-old with a clear path to a starting job is one of the highest-upside assets you can hold.

## The Taxi Squad

New dynasty players often don't understand the taxi squad. Here's how it works in BMFFFL:

- You can hold up to **3 players** on your taxi squad
- Taxi squad players **don't count against your active roster** — they're a development pipeline
- A player is **eligible for taxi** if they are in their first or second NFL season
- Once a player's taxi eligibility expires, they either move to your active roster or are released
- Taxi squad players can be traded

The taxi squad is your farm system. Smart BMFFFL managers use it to stash rookie QBs, developmental WRs, and depth RBs without sacrificing active roster spots.

**Current example**: MLSchools12 has Riley Leonard (QB), Mitchell Evans (TE), and several young WRs on taxi. These players cost nothing now but could be contributors in 2027-2028.

## The Rookie Draft

Every year in June (first Friday), the BMFFFL holds its **4-round linear rookie draft**.

Key details:
- **Linear format**: the same team picks at the same position in every round (pick 1.01 = 1.01, 2.01, 3.01, 4.01)
- **4 rounds, 12 teams**: 48 total picks
- The draft order is determined by **inverse finish order** (worst team picks first)
- Picks can be traded in advance (and they often are — see the trade history)

## Trading in BMFFFL

The BMFFFL has completed **257 all-time trades**. Trading is central to dynasty success.

### What Can Be Traded
- Current players
- Future draft picks (any year, any round)
- Combinations of players and picks

### Trade Tips
- **Don't be afraid to trade players for picks**: in dynasty, picks = future assets
- **Sell veterans at peak value**: a 29-year-old WR1 today may be a depth piece in two years
- **Buy youth**: young players with upside + picks are the currency of dynasty rebuilds
- **Think in multi-year windows**: is this trade better for your 2026 team or your 2028 team?

### Trade Deadline
The BMFFFL trade deadline is **Week 14**. After that, no trades until the offseason.

## Free Agency (FAAB Waivers)

The BMFFFL uses **FAAB (Free Agent Acquisition Budget)** waivers. Each team has **$10,000 per season** to spend on waiver wire acquisitions.

FAAB is blind bidding: you submit bids for available free agents, and the highest bid wins. Unspent FAAB does **not** carry over — it resets to $10,000 each season.

Strategy: FAAB is most valuable early in the season when quality players are dropped. Save significant budget for surprise breakouts and injury replacements.

## How to Think About Your Roster

Dynasty rosters have a **current window** and a **future pipeline**. The best rosters balance both.

### The Win-Now Window
If you have players in their prime (ages 25-28) at key positions, you're in a win-now window. Your job is to maximize your playoff odds in the next 1-3 seasons.

Win-now moves:
- Trade young players and picks for proven veterans
- Target free agents who can contribute immediately
- Don't hold onto injured/declining players out of loyalty

### The Rebuild Mode
If your core is aging or you've missed the playoffs for 2+ years, consider a rebuild.

Rebuild moves:
- Trade aging veterans for young players and picks
- Acquire high draft capital (top-5 picks)
- Be patient — a good rebuild takes 2-3 years

### The Perpetual Contender
The ideal state: always competitive, always reloading. **MLSchools12** has achieved this by combining elite QB play with continuous roster replenishment.

## Common Mistakes for New Dynasty Players

1. **Overvaluing current production, undervaluing age**: A 23-year-old RB2 may be worth more than a 29-year-old RB1.

2. **Hoarding picks indefinitely**: Draft picks are only valuable if you use them well. A perpetual rebuilder who never competes isn't building dynasty wealth — they're just losing with excuses.

3. **Ignoring the taxi squad**: Three free roster spots are a massive advantage. Don't leave them empty.

4. **Undervaluing QBs in Superflex**: In a 12-team SF league, there are only ~18 viable fantasy starters. Every elite QB you acquire is one fewer your opponents can start.

5. **Trading for age**: Acquiring a 30-year-old veteran can help you now but hurts your long-term flexibility.

## TE Premium: Is a First-Round Pick Worth It?

In dynasty fantasy football, tight end is the most polarizing position. The elite TEs — Travis Kelce in his prime, Mark Andrews — produce at a WR1 level. Everyone else is a liability. The gap between TE1 and TE15 is larger than at any other position.

In Superflex leagues like the BMFFFL, this question gets more complicated.

---

## The TE Premium Problem

Standard dynasty analysis says: acquire an elite TE early, pay a premium. The logic is positional scarcity — there are only 2-3 truly elite TEs at any given time, and in a 12-team league, most teams are left with mediocre options.

The counter-argument: in Superflex, QB is even scarcer. With 24 starting QB slots (2 per team × 12 teams), every reasonable starter has value. That QB cost — acquiring a second starting QB — may be more impactful than acquiring an elite TE.

**The BMFFFL perspective**: Look at the current TE landscape:
- MLSchools12: Hunter Henry (age 31, NE). Not elite.
- Tubes94: Likely weak at TE (roster doesn't show a clear TE1)
- Most BMFFFL teams: TE is the weakest position on the roster

This is common in Superflex leagues. Teams allocate their draft capital to QBs and skill players, leaving TE as an afterthought. This creates an opportunity: **an early-2020s elite TE class** that isn't fully accounted for in current BMFFFL rosters.

## The 2026 TE Class

The 2026 NFL Draft has two TEs who could change this calculus:

**Kenyon Sadiq (Oregon)** — Sadiq is the consensus TE1 of the 2026 class. 6'5", 252 lbs, exceptional athleticism, and route-running reminiscent of a WR. Dynasty ADP: 4-6 overall. Projected round 2 NFL pick, likely to a team that will use him immediately.

**Eli Stowers (Vanderbilt)** — The more polarizing prospect. Stowers set a combine record with a 45.5" vertical jump, raising questions about whether he's a pass-catcher or a basketball player wearing football pads. But the production was real: 68 catches, 912 yards, 9 TDs in 2025. Dynasty ADP: 6-9 overall.

If your BMFFFL team needs a TE — and most do — targeting Sadiq or Stowers in rounds 1-2 of the 2026 rookie draft is defensible.

---

## The Math: First-Round Picks and TE Value

Is a first-round rookie pick worth it for a TE?

**The case for paying up**:
- Elite TEs hold value for 8-10 seasons (George Kittle, Travis Kelce)
- TE scarcity in Superflex means an elite TE gives you a weekly edge over 8-10 of your opponents
- The rookie TE learning curve is real, but Sadiq profiles as an immediate contributor

**The case for waiting**:
- First-round picks can produce elite WRs or RBs who have shorter TE replacement cycles
- TE development time means your investment doesn't pay off for 2-3 years
- Adequate TEs can be found in FAAB and trades without giving up a pick

**The verdict**: In a league where most teams are weak at TE, being among the first to acquire an elite TE creates a durable advantage. A 2026 pick 4-6 for Kenyon Sadiq is defensible — not a luxury, but a necessity for teams with a 3-5 year window.

## Taxi Squad Strategy for TEs

Given the development time for TEs, taxi squad usage is critical:

1. **Draft a young TE in rounds 3-4** in addition to any early-round selection
2. **Taxi them for 1-2 years** while they develop in the NFL
3. **Promote to active roster** once they have a defined role

In a 4-round linear draft, there's often value at TE in rounds 3-4. The 2026 class has depth behind Sadiq and Stowers — there may be a TE worth targeting at 3.05-3.10 range for teams that miss on the top guys.

---

## The Verdict

**Should you use a first-round pick on a TE?**

- If your TE room is Hunter Henry, George Kittle, or equivalent aging veterans: **Yes. Target Kenyon Sadiq or Eli Stowers.**
- If you have a young, developing TE already on your roster: **Probably not. Address other needs first.**
- If you're in a win-now window with an 8-6 core: **Consider whether a TE helps you this year. It probably doesn't — look for FAAB or trade options instead.**

The long-term calculus favors paying for elite TEs in dynasty. In a Superflex league, it's not quite as urgent as QB — but when you look at the BMFFFL's collective TE weakness, the 2026 class is as good an entry point as we've had in years.

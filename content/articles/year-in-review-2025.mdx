# Year in Review: The 2025 BMFFFL Season

*March 2026. The dust has settled. Here is what actually happened.*

---

## The Short Version

MLSchools12 went 13-1 in the regular season. Again. They lost in the semifinals. Again. tdtd19844 entered as the 4th seed, knocked off the best team in the league, and won the championship. It was the third time in six seasons that an 8-6 team claimed the title. The BMFFFL has a pattern, and the 2025 season wrote it in bold.

---

## The Regular Season

### MLSchools12: 13-1, The Number That Haunts

By any measurable standard, MLSchools12 owned the 2025 regular season. A 13-1 record is historically rare. In a 12-team, 14-week dynasty league, finishing with one loss means you won games you were supposed to lose. It means your roster had depth, starting-lineup flexibility, and the kind of week-to-week consistency that wins dynasties.

MLSchools12 entered 2025 as the defending champion, coming off a 2024 championship run. The back-to-back was in play. The regular season made the case. Then January came.

### The Rest of the Playoff Field

The playoff picture heading into the postseason shaped up like this:

- **Tubes94** finished 10-4, second-best record in the league. The roster was young and genuinely dangerous. For Tubes94, this was back-to-back double-digit win seasons — an emerging dynasty contender in the making.
- **tdtd19844** finished 8-6 as the 4th seed. No drama, no dominant narrative. Just good enough to get in.
- **JuicyBussy** had another playoff appearance, extending their streak to five in six seasons.
- **Grandes** missed the cut entirely, finishing 4-10 in what became a Moodie Bowl season — a brutal fall from a 2022 championship and a mirror image of the Cogdeill11 arc.

### The Moodie Bowl

Grandes won the 2025 Moodie Bowl (last place), a title that stings differently when you were league champion three years earlier. This is the dynasty lifecycle in its starkest form: a peak window, a collapse, a humiliation. Grandes went from champion to cellar in three years. The 2026 offseason is their inflection point.

## The Playoffs

### Semifinals: The Upset Heard Around the League

The semifinal matchup between MLSchools12 (#1 seed, 13-1) and tdtd19844 (#4 seed, 8-6) was, on paper, one of the most lopsided playoff matchups in BMFFFL history. One team had dominated the entire season. The other had barely squeaked into the field.

tdtd19844 won.

This is now the fourth time in six seasons that the #1 seed has lost in the semifinals. MLSchools12 specifically has a 1-3 semifinal record from the top seed. The "13-1 curse" is no longer a fluke — it is a structural feature of how BMFFFL playoffs work. The highest seed draws the hottest team in the bracket. Teams that enter 8-6 are playing loose, without pressure, with something to prove. The teams that go 13-1 carry the weight of expectation into every playoff game.

The other semifinal sent Tubes94 to the championship game. They were the more convincing winner and entered the final as slight favorites.

### The Championship: tdtd19844 152.92, Tubes94 135.08

The final score tells the story clearly enough. tdtd19844 won 152.92 to 135.08, a margin of 17.84 points — not a blowout, but a comfortable win. Tubes94 had a respectable championship showing for a first appearance. It just wasn't enough.

tdtd19844 became the 2025 BMFFFL champion.

### The 3rd Place Game: Narrowest in League History

The third-place game — the Moodie Bowl consolation between MLSchools12 and whoever Tubes94 beat in the other semi — produced the narrowest margin in BMFFFL postseason history: 0.94 points. A single late-Sunday flex player separating two teams. The specifics are almost unfair. After a 13-1 season, MLSchools12 ended their year on a margin of less than one point.

## The Championship Arc: tdtd19844's Story

The 2025 title was not a fluke. It was the product of a multi-year build that mirrors every successful dynasty arc in this league.

- **2022**: tdtd19844 went 3-11. One of the worst records in the league. The roster was not competitive.
- **2023**: Improvement. Not playoffs, but movement.
- **2024**: More movement. Closer to the field.
- **2025**: 8-6, playoffs, champion.

The three-year rebuild-to-peak trajectory is the BMFFFL standard. Grandes did it (4-9 in 2020, champion in 2022). JuicyBussy did it (missed playoffs in 2020, champion in 2023). tdtd19844 did it. The pattern is not accidental — dynasty football rewards patience, draft capital accumulation, and the willingness to absorb bad seasons while stockpiling young players.

What makes tdtd19844's title interesting is the seed. Winning as a 4th seed with an 8-6 record is different from winning as a dominant team. It means the roster had no single overwhelming player — no massive upside weapon that dragged them to 150+ every week. It means they were balanced. Deep. Consistent across the starting lineup. In dynasty, those are the rosters that sneak up on you.

Tubes94 joined the league in 2021. It took until 2024-2025 for the roster to fully mature. Back-to-back double-digit win seasons, a championship appearance — this is no longer a fringe team. Tubes94 enters 2026 as a legitimate contender, with a roster young enough to sustain the window.

### Cogdeill11's Quiet Season

The 2020 champion has now gone five seasons without a return to the championship. Each year, there's hope. Each year, the results are middling. 2025 continued the trend. Cogdeill11 has the distinction of being a founding champion who has never again been a legitimate contender. The 2026 rookie draft — where they hold the 1.04 — is a genuine opportunity to inject youth and restart the build.

### Escuelas Breaks Through... Sort Of

Escuelas joined the league in 2022 and missed the playoffs in each of their first three seasons. In 2025, they went 6-8 — a career high, but still outside the playoff line. Progress is progress. The trajectory exists. The gap remains.

### The SexMachineAndyD Consistency

Four playoff appearances in six seasons, zero championships. 2024 was a runner-up year. 2025 was another competitive showing. SexMachineAndyD is the definition of a team that is always present but never gets over the hill. It's a frustrating archetype to occupy. The championship-or-bust standard is harsh, and by that measure, four playoff appearances without a ring is a story that hasn't ended yet.

## What 2025 Means for 2026

Every season in dynasty rewrites the league hierarchy. The 2025 season delivered the following verdict:

**The curse is confirmed.** MLSchools12 going 13-1 and losing in the semis is not bad luck anymore. It is their playoff identity. Unless they change something — seed position, roster construction, something — the top-seed exit will continue. The 2021 championship, their dominant year where they cruised to the title, is increasingly the outlier.

**Tubes94 is for real.** Two seasons of double-digit wins, a championship appearance — this team is not a flash in the pan. They enter 2026 as one of the two or three most dangerous rosters in the field.

**Dynasty windows open and close fast.** Grandes from champion to last place in three years. Cogdeill11 from founding champion to six seasons of mediocrity. The window is real. So is what happens when it closes.

**The defending champion is always a target.** No one in six seasons has won back-to-back. History says the league adjusts, the roster ages, and the dynasty pendulum swings. tdtd19844 enters 2026 as the team everyone is trying to beat.

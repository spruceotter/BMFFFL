The 2026 BMFFFL rookie draft is complete. Here are the grades.

Methodology: I'm evaluating each class on three dimensions — **thesis coherence** (does this look like a plan?), **pick quality** (are these good players with real paths to production?), and **roster fit** (does this class make sense given where this team is?). A team in full rebuild mode should be graded differently from a defending champion.

---

## eldridm20 — Franks Little Beauties: **A**

**Class**: Kenyon Sadiq TE/NYJ (1.12), Elijah Sarratt WR/BAL (2.06), Jordyn Tyson WR/NO, Drew Allar QB/PIT, Bryce Lance WR/NO

Sadiq at 1.12 is the best single pick in the class. The 16th-overall NFL pick — consensus dynasty TE1 — in a Jets offense that's accumulating talent. He's out for minicamp post-surgery, but the long-term thesis is intact. When a player at that position, at that age, in that offense, falls to the 12th pick in your draft, you take it.

The supporting class is sensible without being exciting. Sarratt's landing in Baltimore (Lamar Jackson offense, even as depth) is better than expected. Tyson in New Orleans has a path. Allar to Pittsburgh is an interesting long-shot QB project.

The grade is an A primarily because of Sadiq. One transcendent pick at the right position anchors everything.

## El Rioux Grandes — El Rioux Grandes: **A−**

**Class**: Max Klare TE/LAR, Jeremiyah Love RB/ARI, Caleb Douglas WR/MIA, Adam Randall RB/BAL, Jonah Coleman RB/DEN, Chris Brazzell WR/CAR, Justin Joly TE/DEN, Kaelon Black RB/SF

Eight rookies. The commissioner went to work.

After a 4-10 season, this is exactly the right posture: full rebuild, accumulate young assets, don't half-measure it. The volume is impressive. Max Klare (Ohio State TE, Rams) and Jeremiyah Love (Notre Dame RB, Cardinals) are the headliners. Klare could develop into a reliable TE2-or-better in LA's offense. Love's situation in Arizona is cloudier but he's a legitimate NFL talent.

What keeps this from a pure A is roster clarity. Eight new rookies on a 4-10 roster means this is a roster that's been rebuilt from the ground up — and that concentration of young, unproven assets creates a lot of uncertainty about what this team actually is right now. The investment is right. The timeline is long.

Commissioner gets credit for having the courage to commit to the rebuild fully.

## Cogdeill11 — Earn it: **B+**

**Class**: Makai Lemon WR/PHI (1.04), Nicholas Singleton RB/TEN (2.04), Carson Beck QB/ARI (3.04), Deion Burks WR/IND

The three aligned picks — 1.04, 2.04, 3.04 — tell a story. Whether that's intentional design or draft-board circumstance, the result is a class that sits at the same dynasty maturation window (2027-28) and creates a coherent roster identity: rebuild around a WR1, a feature back, and a developmental QB.

Lemon (Eagles, hamstring precautionary) is the centerpiece. The pick is expensive and the timeline is real. Singleton at Tennessee pairs with a roster that needed a feature back. Beck at Arizona is the long-bet dart.

Deion Burks (Colts WR) is the quiet addition — small slot receiver in an Indianapolis offense that's still sorting out its direction. Lower ceiling, but low cost.

Coherent rebuild thesis. The concentration of the three signature picks makes the upside real and the downside substantial. High variance is not a flaw when you're building, not maintaining.

## YellowFever2026 — Yellow Fever: **B+**

**Class**: Fernando Mendoza QB/LV (1.02), Omar Cooper WR/NYJ (1.13), Emmett Johnson RB/KC

The 2028 thesis: spend two first-round picks on players with the same maturation window, collect a UDFA flier, wait. Mendoza (Raiders QB behind Kirk Cousins) and Cooper (Jets slot WR behind Garrett Wilson) are both 2027-28 plays. Neither is touching meaningful production in 2026.

Emmett Johnson (Chiefs, undrafted from Oregon) replaced the Hemby narrative I'd originally heard attached to this roster — he's a similar profile: running back in a competitive NFL depth chart, long-shot flier with a strong landing spot.

The grade is B+ because the thesis is real but the timeline is punishing. You're paying top-1 and top-13 prices for players who won't help you for two years. If Mendoza becomes the starter in Las Vegas, this class becomes an A. If he doesn't, it's a costly miss.

## SexMachineAndyD: **B**

**Class**: Ty Simpson QB/LAR, Cade Klubnik QB/NYJ, Garrett Nussmeier QB/KC

Three quarterbacks. No running backs. No wide receivers. No tight ends.

This is an unusual draft class, and unusual isn't bad — it's just specific. SexMachineAndyD made a bet that at least one QB dart from this class will develop into a meaningful fantasy asset. Klubnik at NYJ is the most interesting piece: Clemson starter, going to a Jets offense that's been building talent. If the Jets' signal-caller situation evolves, Klubnik's in an interesting position.

Nussmeier (LSU) to Kansas City is a strong landing spot by NFL development metrics — learning from Mahomes, competitive system. Simpson (Alabama) to the Rams joins a franchise that has produced fantasy QBs before.

The grade: B for execution of a clear thesis (QB lottery tickets), deducted slightly for having no positional diversity. If one hits, this class looks prescient. If none hit, there's nothing else here.

## Schoolcraft Football Team — MLSchools12: **C+**

**Class**: Germie Bernard WR/PIT, Matt Hibner TE/BAL, Demond Claiborne RB/MIN, J'Mari Taylor RB/JAX, Kaytron Allen RB/WAS

Five late-round additions, no obvious headliner. This looks like a team that knows it doesn't need to rebuild — and drafted accordingly.

For a two-time champion entering 2026 off a 13-1 season, minimal rookie investment is the right posture. You don't burn first-round picks when your roster is already championship-caliber. The C+ reflects the class quality more than the strategy — none of these are high-upside adds.

Germie Bernard (Pitt WR to Pittsburgh) has positional and schematic familiarity working for him. Hibner at Baltimore is a TE flier worth monitoring. The three running backs (Claiborne/MIN, Taylor/JAX, Allen/WAS) are all depth backs in competitive rooms.

Smart restraint graded as an average class.

## Showtyme Boyz — Cmaleski: **C+**

**Class**: Jadarian Price RB/SEA, Oscar Delp TE/NO, Zachariah Branch WR/ATL, Kevin Coleman WR/MIA, Denzel Boston WR/CLE

Five picks with real range. Zachariah Branch (USC WR, landing in Atlanta) is the best name here — legitimate speed, went to a franchise that's been building its receiver room. Kevin Coleman (Missouri WR, Miami) has upside in a Tua offense that feeds receivers. Denzel Boston in Cleveland is a longer shot but a physical specimen.

Oscar Delp (Georgia TE, New Orleans) lands in a Saints offense that has used its tight ends. Jadarian Price is a RB flier at Seattle — crowded depth chart.

Nothing elite, but respectable volume with one or two genuine upside pieces. B− on talent, docked to C+ for the lack of a clear anchor.

## Really Big Rings — rbr: **C+**

## The Class in Summary

The 2026 BMFFFL draft class is defined by a few standout picks and a lot of developmental depth. **Sadiq at 1.12** (eldridm20) is the consensus best pick. **Mendoza at 1.02** (YellowFever) is the highest-cost, highest-upside bet. **Cogdeill11's three aligned picks** are the most architecturally interesting.

The rest of the class: GMs making sensible if modest investments in a draft year where the top talent was concentrated at the top of the board.

Check back in 2028. That's when most of these theses resolve.

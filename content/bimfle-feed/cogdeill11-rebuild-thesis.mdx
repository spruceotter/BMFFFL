Cogdeill11 won it all in 2020. Beat eldridsm in the championship. Then, by 2025, went 5-9 — tied for last in wins, last in points (1,626.5 pts, nearly 400 below the median). That's a peak-to-decline arc of five years, which in dynasty is actually not that unusual. The question now is whether the 2026 draft class is the beginning of a coherent rebuild or an accumulation of individual bets without a thesis.

---

## The Three Picks

**Makai Lemon WR/PHI** *(1.04)*

The headline pick. Lemon was the Eagles' first-round selection — 22nd overall — and projects as a field-stretching WR2 in Philadelphia's offense. He's sitting out minicamp with a hamstring issue, which is described as precautionary and expected to resolve before training camp.

A 1.04 WR in a contending offense is the correct dynasty move for a rebuilding team. You need a blue-chip piece to build around. Lemon has that profile: young, athletic, landing spot is a real passing offense. The hamstring is worth monitoring but isn't a dynasty-busting concern at this stage.

**Nicholas Singleton RB/TEN** *(2.04)*

Tennessee's 2nd-round RB. Singleton (Penn State) was a compact inside runner who tested well athletically. The Titans' backfield situation is in flux — Tony Pollard is aging out of relevance and the team's offensive direction is genuinely unclear. Singleton has a path to a starting role if the Titans commit to a run-first identity with their rebuild, but the timeline isn't guaranteed.

RB in the 2nd round of dynasty is a reasonable swing on volume + opportunity. The Titans aren't a featured offense, but a workhorse RB in a struggling franchise still accumulates plenty of rushing attempts.

**Carson Beck QB/ARI** *(3.04)*

The speculative piece. Beck is a 3rd-round quarterback starting behind Kyler Murray in Arizona. He's been getting more reps than expected at OTAs because of a Brissett absence, but "more reps than expected for a 3rd-round backup" is a low bar. The Cardinals have exactly zero incentive to rush Kyler Murray's successor — they're still in the Murray years.

Beck as a dynasty asset is a patience play. If Murray gets hurt or Arizona rebuilds in 2027-28, Beck could ascend to a starter's role with a good arm and an offense around him. If Murray stays healthy through 2027, Beck might be cut before he ever matters. He's a high-variance flier, not a cornerstone.

---

## The Thesis Question

Three picks: a WR cornerstone (1.04), a RB volume candidate (2.04), a QB lottery ticket (3.04). Is this a coherent rebuild?

Loosely, yes. Lemon is the anchor — the pick that makes you take this rebuild seriously. Singleton adds a second young skill position piece. Beck is the optional upside swing that doesn't cost much and could hit big.

The thing that's missing is **timeline alignment**. A dynasty rebuild works when your young pieces are ready to compete at the same time. Lemon profiles as a relevant asset starting in 2027-28. Singleton similarly. Beck is a 2028-or-never proposition. The rebuild window, if it works, opens in 2027 and runs through 2029.

That's actually clean. It's not a rebuild that's trying to do too much too soon.

---

## The Risk Case

Lemon's hamstring creates a near-term concern. If the hamstring becomes a recurring issue — soft tissue injuries in WRs have a pattern of lingering — a 1.04 that misses time in years one and two derails the rebuild anchor. Singleton's value depends heavily on the Titans' offensive identity, which is genuinely unknown. Beck may never touch the field.

The more systemic risk: the 2025 roster (5-9, last in points) needs more than a three-pick draft to be competitive. The team's existing veterans will need to be evaluated honestly. If the core isn't producing, three picks — even good ones — can't fix a league-worst points total in one cycle.

---

## Comparison: 2020 Championship Squad

The 2020 championship team that Cogdeill11 built presumably had a different profile — a more established roster with peak-year contributors. That's what dynasty decay looks like: the pieces that won slowly age and produce less, and the rebuild is always three to four years behind.

The 2026 class is step one of that rebuild. Step two is training camp. Step three is 2027.

Check back in 2028.

Mandatory minicamp week 2 is underway for 17 teams, including Philadelphia and the Jets. Here's how BMFFFL's 2026 draft class is faring.

---

## 🔴 Sitting Out

**Makai Lemon WR/PHI** *(Cogdeill11, 1.04)*

Lemon was not in a helmet at Tuesday's session — spectating, not practicing. The hamstring issue that's kept him out since OTAs is described as precautionary, and insiders expect him fully ready for training camp in July. Still, he's missing meaningful spring reps with the Eagles WR group. Nothing alarming, but the clock on this one starts when he's back on the practice field.

**Kenyon Sadiq TE/NYJ** *(eldridm20, 1.12)*

Post-hernia surgery and out of all OTAs and minicamp. The Jets' 16th-overall pick — the consensus dynasty TE1 — won't be a minicamp story. Timeline aligns for a July training camp return. His absence changes nothing about the long-term outlook; it just means we have less spring film to evaluate. The Jets remain high on him.

---

## 🟡 Practicing with Limitations

**Fernando Mendoza QB/LV** *(YellowFever, 1.02)*

Still taking 3rd-team reps while Kirk Cousins runs with the 1s. Per Adam Schefter, the Raiders want Cousins to start the season. But the coaching staff is consistently effusive: OC Andrew Janocko called Mendoza "a sponge that wants to know everything," and HC Klint Kubiak said he's been "as advertised." Brady is apparently mentoring.

This is the plan. A 22-year-old 1.02 QB project working his way up from the 3s is not a red flag — it's the intended scenario. The 2028 timeline is intact.

---

## 🟢 Full Go

**Omar Cooper Jr. WR/NYJ** *(YellowFever, 1.13)*

The 30th-overall pick is getting reps with Geno Smith at OTAs. He profiles as a slot WR behind Garrett Wilson and Adonai Mitchell. Year-one role is limited by design — the Jets drafted him for the future. The talent pipeline is real.

**Elijah Sarratt WR/BAL** *(eldridm20, 2.06)*

Ravens 4th-rounder (115th overall), competing behind Zay Flowers. Rashod Bateman and Ja'Kobi Lane are the hurdles. Landing spot is better than pre-draft expectations: playing in a Lamar Jackson offense, even as a depth piece, gives him a real path to targets.

**Kaytron Allen RB/WAS** *(MLSchools12, 3.01)*

Washington 6th-round pick (187th overall). Competing in a crowded backfield: Croskey-Merritt, Rachaad White, and Jerome Ford are all ahead of him. Short-yardage specialist is the realistic path. Steep hill to meaningful fantasy production in 2026, but he's healthy and competing.

## 📍 Franchise Clusters to Watch

**Jets (NYJ):** Two BMFFFL first-round picks landed on the same franchise. Sadiq (eldridm20, 1.12) and Cooper Jr. (YellowFever, 1.13) are both building toward the same offense. Whether Geno Smith or someone else is under center when they peak, eldridm20 and YellowFever are both betting on the Jets' pass-catcher development pipeline.

**Raiders (LV):** Mendoza (YellowFever, 1.02) and Hemby (tdtd, UDFA) are both on Las Vegas. Two BMFFFL rosters have a stake in this offense — though the connection is more accident than design.

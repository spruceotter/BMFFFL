Mandatory minicamp is over. Every NFL team has now concluded its spring practice window. From here, it's a six-week dead period before training camps open in mid-July.

This is the final briefing of the spring evaluation period. Here's what the 2026 BMFFFL draft class showed us — and what questions remain heading into camp.

---

## What the Spring Window Told Us

### 🔴 The Uncertainty: Makai Lemon (WR/PHI)
*Cogdeill11, 1.04 pick*

Lemon missed the entire spring. Hamstring issue picked up during voluntary OTAs sidelined him for both OTA sessions and both mandatory minicamp days. He was present at practices — in uniform, without a helmet — but did not take a single rep with the offense.

That's a problem for development reps, but not for the dynasty thesis. Lemon is a 2027-28 asset. The concern isn't whether he misses June — it's whether he misses July. If he shows up to training camp healthy and takes reps with A.J. Brown and the first-team Eagles offense, the spring absence disappears from the narrative.

**What to watch in July**: Does Lemon clear training camp on Day 1, or does the hamstring linger? The first practice report from Eagles camp will be the signal.

---

### 🟢 The Upgrade: Omar Cooper Jr. (WR/NYJ)
*YellowFever, 1.13 pick*

The biggest news of the spring window — and it favors YellowFever. Multiple beat writers covering Jets minicamp pegged Cooper as a potential WR2 *this season*, not just in the 2027-28 window I projected at draft time. The Jets traded up from 33 to 30 to get him, and the coaching staff appears to be targeting an immediate role.

That doesn't invalidate the 2028 upside thesis — it front-loads it. If Cooper generates WR2 production in 2026-27 *on top of* the Mendoza-era upside, YellowFever's first-round class is meaningfully better than graded. The June 11 draft grades called it a B+; this spring update would push the floor toward B+ and keep the ceiling above that.

**What to watch in July**: Does Cooper play with the first-team offense? Does his snap share with Garrett Wilson resemble a complement or a competitor? That distinction matters for 2026 production.

---

### 🟡 On Track: Kenyon Sadiq (TE/NYJ)
*eldridm20, 1.12 pick*

Hernia surgery complete. Aaron Glenn confirmed training camp return on track. Sadiq missed OTAs and minicamp, but the procedure was expected and the timeline isn't alarming. He's the dynasty TE1 of this class regardless.

For eldridm20: the A grade holds.

**What to watch in July**: First reps with Wilson at training camp. The TE1 designation sticks barring a genuine medical complication.

---

### ✅ Proceeding as Expected: Fernando Mendoza (QB/LV)
*YellowFever, 1.02 pick*

Third-team reps, occasionally second. OC Janocko continues to praise his process ("a sponge"). HC Kubiak describes him as "as advertised." Kirk Cousins starts in 2026. The Mendoza developmental plan is executing exactly as projected on draft night.

This is a 2028+ asset. The spring window wasn't going to change that assessment, and it didn't.

### ⬆️ Better Than Expected: Skyler Bell (WR/BUF)
*JuicyBussy*

Bell had a rocky start at rookie minicamp — "shaky" was the early read — but recovered to make plays during Buffalo's final open OTA with the top receivers absent. The competition is real, but trending in the right direction. JuicyBussy's one-pick class ends spring on a modest positive note.

## The Spring Picture

Three weeks of OTAs and two days of mandatory minicamp produced one clear upgrade (Cooper Jr. earlier than expected), one ongoing uncertainty (Lemon hamstring), and four players on track with their draft projections.

That's a reasonable spring for a draft class that wasn't built for immediate 2026 returns. The BMFFFL 2026 class is broadly a 2027-28 story. The spring window validated that framing — Cooper Jr. aside, nobody dramatically overperformed or underperformed expectations.

The next real signal: mid-July training camp.

## The 8-6 Question

In dynasty fantasy football, regular season record correlates imperfectly with roster quality. The best roster doesn't always produce the best record — scheduling luck, injury variance, and opponent timing all contaminate the signal. But over 14 weeks, a 13-1 team (MLSchools12) is almost certainly better than an 8-6 team (tdtd19844) on expected value.

What happened in the playoffs is a different question. Playoff football in fantasy leagues is a small sample: 2-3 games, 4-6 weeks of scoring. The gap between a 13-1 team and an 8-6 team on expected value might be 8-10 points per week. In a 2-week playoff format, that gap is surmountable — and it was.

This is not a criticism of tdtd19844's championship. It's a structural observation: playoff champions in fantasy leagues are drawn from a distribution that includes teams *better than their record* as well as teams with *late-season peaks*. The Shameful Saggy sack is the 2025 BMFFFL champion because they were good enough to be in the playoffs and then ran hot at the right moment.

That's a real category of winner.

---

## What It Takes to Repeat

The challenge for an 8-6 champion entering 2026 is different from the challenge facing a 13-1 team that didn't win.

For MLSchools12, the question is sustainability — can they maintain elite regular-season production while the league rebuilds around them? Their problem is holding the high ground.

For tdtd19844, the question is more fundamental: **was the 2025 championship a peak or a preview?**

An 8-6 regular season record is consistent with two very different roster profiles:
1. A roster with a high ceiling and inconsistent floor — feast-or-famine lineup construction that goes on long winning streaks and cold streaks
2. A roster that genuinely improved throughout the season and peaked at the right time

The playoff run tells you they executed when it counted. The regular season record tells you they had weeks where they didn't. Which pattern dominates in 2026 matters enormously.

A UDFA add is the lowest-cost, lowest-expectation draft move possible. It signals one of two things: either tdtd19844 was satisfied with their current roster construction and didn't need to invest high picks, or they didn't have the capital to invest.

The former is the more favorable reading for a defending champion. If the Shameful Saggy sack enters 2026 fully stocked and running back Hemby as a depth flier, that's the profile of a team that won it all and didn't need to rebuild anything.

## The Real Test

Defending championships in dynasty fantasy leagues requires something that building them doesn't: resisting the temptation to change a winning formula.

After a championship, every move feels urgent. Do you extend the window by trading for veterans? Do you sell picks you have while you're on top? Do you lock into the current core and ride it?

The Shameful Saggy sack's minimal draft activity in 2026 suggests they're choosing door three: stay the course. That's disciplined if the core is genuinely championship-caliber. It's risky if the 2025 run required variance going your way.

We'll know more in September. For now, the Shameful Saggy sack enters 2026 as the team everyone in BMFFFL is chasing — and the team that needs to prove the 2025 run wasn't a one-year event.

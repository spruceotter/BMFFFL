# 2016 Season: The Inaugural Year

*The BMFFFL began on ESPN with an auction draft, twelve owners, and Antonio Brown going for $314. MLSchools12 won the first championship and set a standard that would haunt the league for years.*

---

## The Season at a Glance

The 2016 season was the founding year of BMFFFL — the first of four seasons on ESPN before the league migrated to Sleeper in 2020. The format was auction draft, which put a premium on roster construction skill from the very first pick. Antonio Brown at $314 was the marquee acquisition of the draft, and whoever held him had a weapon; whoever overpaid elsewhere was squeezed all year.

**MLSchools12 (The Murder Boners) were simply the best team.** 11-2 in the regular season, 1,759.8 points, top seed, and then a clean playoff run to the championship. They won by 19.44 points over Grandes (El Rioux Grandes) in the title game. The BMFFFL dynasty had its first champion.

The most notable second-place regular-season story: Grandes actually scored 1,742.0 points — higher than 9 of the 12 teams — yet finished 8-5. rbr and mmoodie12 both outscored their records too. Scheduling variance was a feature of BMFFFL from the first season.

## Champion: MLSchools12 (The Murder Boners)

**Record**: 11-2 (1st seed) | **Championship score**: 156.0

The Murder Boners were the class of the inaugural BMFFFL season. The auction format rewarded exactly the kind of roster construction that MLSchools12 executed: elite anchor players, not just one bloated acquisition. They led the league in wins, went through the playoffs cleanly, and won by comfortable margin.

This championship established a pattern that would define BMFFFL history: MLSchools12 as the perpetual measuring stick. Every other team's season, in some sense, became measured against whether they could beat the Murder Boners.

---

## Key Narratives

**The Auction Draft Era begins.**
ESPN's auction format put a hard test on valuation skills from day one. $314 for Antonio Brown was the headline number. Teams that spent efficiently had roster depth; teams that overspent on a few stars got hurt by injury or variance. MLSchools12 apparently threaded the needle.

**Points don't always mean wins.**
The top three scorers by total points were MLSchools12 (1st, 11-2), Grandes (3rd by record, 8-5), and rbr (7th by record, 7-6). The correlation between points and wins was already noisy in year one. mmoodie12 scored 1,712.8 points and finished 6-7.

**The first dynasty claim.**
Cogdeill11 finished 5-8 in the inaugural season — 9th place. Three years later, in 2017, they would win the championship from a 7-6 regular-season record. Origin stories are not always obvious.

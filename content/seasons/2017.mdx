# 2017 Season: The Great Upset

*The greatest upset in BMFFFL history. A 7-6 team beat an 11-2 team in the championship, while the best team in the league — 12-1 — watched from the couch. The lowest-scoring final ever sealed it.*

---

## The Season at a Glance

The 2017 season produced the most improbable result in BMFFFL history. MLSchools12 went 12-1 — the best single-season record the ESPN era had seen — and didn't win the championship. eldridsm went 11-2 and was the clear second-best team in the league, a heavy favorite in the title game. Cogdeill11 went 7-6, snuck into the 6th seed, and beat them all.

The championship score — 109.32 to 89.54 — remains the lowest-scoring final in BMFFFL records. Cogdeill11 didn't win it with a blowout. They won it with survival. eldridsm scored 89 points in the most important game of the season. That's how it goes sometimes.

**The central irony of 2017**: the league's two best regular-season teams (12-1 and 11-2) both lost. The champion finished 7-6.

## Champion: Cogdeill11 (Team Cogdeill)

**Record**: 7-6 (6th seed) | **Championship score**: 109.32

Cogdeill11 entered the playoffs as the 6th and final seed with a 7-6 record. Three weeks later they were champions. They beat the 3rd seed in the wild card, beat the 2nd seed in the semis, then won the final in a low-scoring grinder.

The 2016 season had seen Cogdeill11 finish 5-8 and miss the playoffs entirely. The 2017 version came back, made it in by the skin of their teeth, and ran the table. The championship was legitimate — every win was earned on the field — but it stands as the clearest demonstration in BMFFFL history that regular-season record and playoff performance are weakly correlated once you're in.

## Key Narratives

**The 12-1 curse begins.**
MLSchools12 went 12-1 in the regular season and did not win the championship. This is the founding moment of what would become the central BMFFFL narrative: the best team losing when it mattered most. The pattern established in 2017 repeated in 2018, 2020, and beyond.

**The greatest upset.**
A 7-6 team beating an 11-2 team in the championship game is, by any reasonable measure, the most improbable result in BMFFFL history. Cogdeill11 in 2017 is the definitive Cinderella story of the ESPN era.

**The lowest-scoring final.**
109.32-89.54 is a number that defines a certain kind of playoff chaos — the kind where both teams underperform on the biggest stage and the trophy goes to whoever holds together slightly better. Cogdeill11 held together.

**Five teams finished 4-9 or worse.**
The bottom half of 2017 was a massacre. JuicyBussy, mmoodie12, tyler_drysdale, and dan_gaudy all went 4-9. eldridm20 went 2-11. The league had a pronounced gap between the top six teams and the rest.

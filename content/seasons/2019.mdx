# 2019 Season: The Last ESPN Season

*MLSchools12 went 12-1, scored 197.98 in the championship — the highest in ESPN-era BMFFFL history — and closed the book on four years of ESPN. Several members left after this season. The league moved to Sleeper in 2020.*

---

## The Season at a Glance

The 2019 season was the last BMFFFL season on ESPN, and it ended with a statement. MLSchools12 went 12-1, led the league with 2,219.0 points — a dominant regular season by any measure — then scored 197.98 in the championship game to win by 33 points. The highest championship score in ESPN-era BMFFFL history. A clean close to the chapter.

The runner-up tells a different story. rbr (Really Big Rings) finished the regular season 6-7. Sixth seed. By most metrics, not a championship contender. They made the final anyway — the most improbable runner-up in the ESPN era. They lost by 33 points, but they were there.

After 2019, several long-time members departed: tyler_drysdale, dan_gaudy, mmoodie12, and JuicyBussy (Matt DeLaura) all left the league. The roster that launched BMFFFL on ESPN was breaking up. The move to Sleeper in 2020 wasn't just a platform change — it was a reconstitution of the league itself.

## Key Narratives

**The highest-scoring ESPN championship.**
197.98 is the number. No other ESPN-era championship came close. mlschools12 didn't just win the final — they set a record doing it.

**The most improbable runner-up in ESPN history.**
rbr finished 6-7 in the regular season and reached the championship game. They scored 1,903.7 points on the year — enough to be a top-3 scorer — but their record kept them seeded 6th. They won three playoff games to get to the final. The scheduling gods had denied them all year, and the playoff gods almost made it right.

**The end of an era — literally.**
The 2019 season was the last on ESPN. After 2019, tyler_drysdale, dan_gaudy, mmoodie12, and JuicyBussy left the league. New members joined. The platform shifted to Sleeper. When the 2020 season started, BMFFFL was a different league in nearly every way — same core, new context. The ESPN era closed cleanly, with the founding champion also holding the final ESPN-era trophy.

**The dynasty picture.**
Across four ESPN seasons, three different teams won championships: MLSchools12 (2016, 2019), Cogdeill11 (2017), and SexMachineAndyD (2018). Two of those three (Cogdeill11 and SexMachineAndyD) won from outside the top two seeds. Only MLSchools12 won as a 1-seed. The ESPN era rewarded chaos as much as talent.

## Detailed Findings

### 1. Cam Ward — QB, Tennessee Titans

**Draft:** #1 overall pick, 2025 NFL Draft (as expected)

**2025 Regular Season Stats:**
- Games Started: 17 (all 17 regular season games)
- Completions/Attempts: 323/540
- Completion %: 59.8%
- Passing Yards: 3,169
- Passing TDs: 15
- Interceptions: 7
- Rushing Attempts: 39
- Rushing Yards: 159
- Rushing TDs: 2
- Fumbles Lost: 7

**Starter Status:** Yes — Ward was the full-season starter, starting all 17 games. He exited the Week 18 game early (the team's optional 18th game in a loss at Jacksonville) with a right shoulder AC joint sprain. That game is not counted in official regular season totals.

**Context:** Head coach Brian Callahan was fired after six games, disrupting his rookie year. Ward posted a 10:1 TD:INT ratio over the final eight weeks after the team's bye. He took the most sacks of any NFL QB in 2025 (48). PFF gave him an overall grade of 58.5 (39th out of 43 qualifying QBs). QBR of 33.2.

### 2. Travis Hunter — WR/CB, Jacksonville Jaguars

**Draft:** #2 overall pick, 2025 NFL Draft
Drafted by the **Jacksonville Jaguars**, NOT another team. The Jaguars traded up from #5 to #2 overall, sending #5, #36 (Round 2), #126 (Round 4), and their 2026 first-round pick to the Cleveland Browns.

**2025 Regular Season Stats (WR):**
- Games Played: 7 (season ended on IR)
- Targets: 45
- Receptions: 28
- Receiving Yards: 298
- Receiving TDs: 1
- Also recorded: 15 tackles and 3 passes defensed (CB role)

**Injury Note:** Hunter suffered a non-contact LCL injury to his right knee during practice and was placed on injured reserve on October 31, 2025. Season-ending surgery was required. He is expected to be ready for 2026 training camp (approximately 6-month recovery).

### 3. Ashton Jeanty — RB, Las Vegas Raiders

**Draft:** #6 overall pick, 2025 NFL Draft (highest RB drafted since Saquon Barkley at #2 in 2018; highest for Raiders since Darren McFadden at #4 in 2008)

**2025 Regular Season Stats:**
- Games Played: 17
- Rushing Attempts: 266
- Rushing Yards: 975
- Rushing TDs: 5
- Yards Per Carry: 3.7
- Receptions: 49
- Receiving Yards: 266
- Receiving TDs: 4
- Total Yards from Scrimmage: 1,321

**Context:** Led all 2025 NFL rookies in rushing yards. Became the first Raiders rookie with 300+ scrimmage touches in a season, and only the second rookie league-wide this decade (joining Najee Harris, 2021). Advanced stats showed he ranked 2nd in broken tackles (16) and 7th in yards after contact (321) among RBs. The Raiders' offensive line and poor team record (they were frequently playing from behind) limited his efficiency (3.7 YPC).

### 4. Tetairoa McMillan — WR, Carolina Panthers

**Draft:** #8 overall pick, 2025 NFL Draft (from University of Arizona, 6'5", 212 lbs)

**2025 Regular Season Stats:**
- Games Played: 17
- Targets: 122 (12th in NFL)
- Receptions: 70
- Receiving Yards: 1,014
- Receiving TDs: 7
- Yards Per Reception: 14.5

**Playoff Stats:** 5 rec on 7 targets for 81 yards in Wild Card loss to the Los Angeles Rams.

**Awards:** Won the **2025 NFL Offensive Rookie of the Year** award. Set the Panthers franchise rookie receiving yards record. Was the only 2025 rookie WR to reach 1,000 receiving yards. Named top WR on the 2025 NFL All-Rookie Team. Five of his seven TDs came from Week 11 onwards.

## Validation Notes for Dynasty Website

- **Cam Ward** data appears consistent across sources. Full-season starter confirmed. Stats are modest for a #1 pick but understandable given coaching change and poor OL.
- **Travis Hunter** — key correction flag: ensure the site lists his team as the **Jacksonville Jaguars**, not any other team. He was pick #2, not a later pick. His stats are limited due to the IR injury after just 7 games.
- **Ashton Jeanty** — stats confirmed. 975 rush yards / 5 rush TD / 49 rec / 266 rec yds / 4 rec TD. Total scrimmage yards: 1,321.
- **Tetairoa McMillan** — stats confirmed. Offensive Rookie of the Year. Strong full-season performer; 1,014 yards / 7 TD is dynasty-relevant production.

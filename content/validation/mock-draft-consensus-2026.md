## CRITICAL FINDING: CLASS YEAR MISMATCH

**The players named in the article research brief — Cam Ward, Travis Hunter, Tetairoa McMillan, Ashton Jeanty, Shedeur Sanders, Omarion Hampton, Colston Loveland, Harold Fannin Jr., Quinshon Judkins, Tyler Warren, Luther Burden III, Matthew Golden, Jayden Higgins, Jack Bech, RJ Harvey — are all members of the 2025 NFL Draft class.** They entered the NFL in 2025 and have already completed their rookie seasons. They are NOT the 2026 NFL Draft class.

The 2026 NFL Draft class (players entering the NFL in 2026) is a completely different group led by **Jeremiyah Love (RB, Notre Dame)**, **Carnell Tate (WR, Ohio State)**, **Makai Lemon (WR, USC)**, **Jordyn Tyson (WR, Arizona State)**, and **Fernando Mendoza (QB, Indiana)**.

This mismatch is the most significant finding of this research. Any article on the BMFFFL website that presents Cam Ward, Travis Hunter, Tetairoa McMillan, etc. as "2026 NFL Draft prospects" or "upcoming rookies" contains a fundamental factual error.

## Part 3: Key Consensus Takeaways — March 2026

### Who is the 2026 Dynasty Rookie Draft 1.01?
**Jeremiyah Love (RB, Notre Dame)** is the unambiguous, near-universal 1.01 in all 1QB dynasty rookie drafts as of March 2026. In superflex formats, **Fernando Mendoza (QB, Indiana)** and Love compete for the top spot depending on league context.

### Is Tetairoa McMillan or Travis Hunter Ranked #1 in Dynasty ADP?
**Neither.** Both are 2025 NFL Draft players who have already played their rookie year. In terms of *current dynasty value*, McMillan is preferred over Hunter in offense-only leagues (Hunter had a knee injury and two-way usage risk). The question of "dynasty ADP #1 vs. #2" between them is from the **pre-2025 season** debate, not March 2026 current state.

For the actual 2026 draft class, the WR1 by dynasty ADP is **Carnell Tate**, with Makai Lemon close behind (some sources flip them).

### Where do TEs Rank Relative to Each Other?

**2025 class TEs (already in NFL):**
- Colston Loveland, Tyler Warren, and Harold Fannin Jr. are considered a mega top tier together — all three are high-upside dynasty assets heading into Year 2, with CBS Sports grouping them in the same tier for 2026 dynasty. Loveland narrowly leads on most boards by overall pick position.

**2026 draft class TEs (incoming rookies):**
- **Kenyon Sadiq (Oregon)** is the consensus TE1, ranking as high as #4 overall in some mock drafts. He is expected to be the only TE taken in the first 40 rookie draft picks.
- **Eli Stowers (Vanderbilt)** is the TE2 and a major post-combine riser; some analysts have him cracking the top 15 overall.
- No other 2026 TE is near their level.

### Sleepers Outside the Top 10 (2026 Draft Class)
- **Omar Cooper Jr. (WR, Indiana):** Major combine riser; some boards have him in top-9 consensus picks despite ranking outside that range pre-combine. Good hands, high ceiling.
- **Eli Stowers (TE, Vanderbilt):** Undervalued on KTC (TE25 dynasty) despite near-universal analyst praise after the combine; 1st Team All-SEC, Mackey Award.
- **Zachariah Branch (WR, USC):** 4.35 combine speed, rose to WR8 on post-combine boards; 5'10"/180 size is the concern.
- **Mike Washington Jr. (RB, Arkansas):** Only RB to run sub-4.35 at 223 lbs at combine; athletic RB2 candidate in a thin class.

### Prospects the Dynasty Community is Fading
- **Shedeur Sanders (QB, Browns):** Fell to the 5th round of the 2025 NFL Draft despite being a presumed high pick. Ranked 34th overall post-draft by CBS Sports. This is the clearest example of a dynasty community fade for a player once expected to be a top-5 pick.
- **Travis Hunter (WR/CB, Jaguars):** Labeled a dynasty **sell** by Pro Football Network in February 2026 due to knee injury and two-way usage risk (Pro Football Network, February 2026).
- **Denzel Boston (WR, Washington, 2026 class):** Some concern about route-running polish; at least one outlet ranks below consensus.

## Part 5: Significant Omissions from Prospect Coverage

If the website intended to cover the **2026 NFL Draft class** (actual incoming rookies), the following top prospects are likely missing or underrepresented:

1. **Jeremiyah Love (RB, Notre Dame)** — Universal 1.01 dynasty pick; the most important player in the entire 2026 class
2. **Carnell Tate (WR, Ohio State)** — Consensus WR1 of 2026 class; top-10 NFL Draft pick
3. **Jordyn Tyson (WR, Arizona State)** — Top-3 WR in 2026 class; highest ceiling WR if healthy
4. **Kenyon Sadiq (TE, Oregon)** — Consensus TE1 of 2026 class; 41.5" vertical; top-6 dynasty pick
5. **Fernando Mendoza (QB, Indiana)** — Heisman winner; QB1 of 2026 class; projected #1 overall NFL pick (Raiders)
6. **Eli Stowers (TE, Vanderbilt)** — Major combine riser; TE2 of class; Mackey Award winner
7. **Jonah Coleman (RB, Washington)** — RB2 candidate in thin RB class
8. **KC Concepcion (WR, Texas A&M)** — Late-1st dynasty pick; consistent top-10 appearance in mock drafts
9. **Jadarian Price (RB, Notre Dame)** — Second Notre Dame RB; breakaway speed; dynasty depth

If the website intended to cover the **2025 NFL Draft class** (which appears to be the actual subject based on the player names), the framing must change: these players are not "prospects" or "draft rankings" — they are current NFL players with established (if short) NFL track records, and they should be covered with dynasty hold/buy/sell framing, not pre-draft prospect analysis.

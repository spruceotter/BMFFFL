# 2026 NFL Draft Prospect Fact-Check
**Date:** March 15, 2026
**Purpose:** Verify accuracy of prospect data for BMFFFL dynasty fantasy football website
**Draft Date:** April 24-26, 2026 (Note: multiple sources cite April 23-25; verify official date)

---

## Summary of Findings

Of the 10 prospects checked, **7 have confirmed errors or discrepancies** in the data provided. Three prospects are largely accurate. Details below.

---

## Wide Receivers

### Carnell Tate (Ohio State)
**Status: MOSTLY ACCURATE — minor discrepancy on weight and drop claim wording**

- **College:** Ohio State — CONFIRMED
- **2026 Draft:** CONFIRMED, declared for draft
- **Height 6'3":** CONFIRMED (measured 6'3" at combine, some sources round to 6'2")
- **Weight 195 lbs:** DISCREPANCY — measured at **192 lbs** at the 2026 NFL Combine; listed at 193 on roster. 195 is inaccurate.
- **Projected top-10 pick:** CONFIRMED — ranked No. 7-8 on multiple big boards (Kiper, Jeremiah), widely projected WR1
- **"Zero drops in tight coverage":** MISLEADING/IMPRECISE — PFF credits him with **0 drops in the 2025 season** (drop rate 0.0%) and 12/14 contested catches (85.7%). However, his career drop rate is ~4.0% with five career drops; one scout contested the zero-drop charting. The claim "zero drops in tight coverage" is defensible for the 2025 season but should be attributed to that season specifically, not as a general career trait.

**Verdict:** Weight is wrong (192 lbs, not 195 lbs). Drop claim needs context (2025 season only).

### Makai Lemon (USC)
**Status: TWO ERRORS — weight and drop rate are wrong**

- **College:** USC — CONFIRMED
- **2026 Draft:** CONFIRMED, declared for draft
- **Height 5'11":** CONFIRMED (measured 5'11" at combine)
- **Weight 195 lbs:** DISCREPANCY — measured at **192 lbs** at the 2026 NFL Combine (some sources say 192, others 192.4)
- **Biletnikoff Award winner 2025:** CONFIRMED — won the Fred Biletnikoff Award (nation's top WR) for the 2025 season
- **21.3% drop rate:** WRONG — Lemon's drop rate was approximately **2.5%** in 2025 (2 drops on 108 targets per PFF). His career drop rate over his final two seasons was also extremely low (~2.3%). A 21.3% drop rate would be catastrophically bad and is the opposite of reality. This is a severe factual error.

**Verdict:** Weight slightly off (192 not 195). Drop rate of 21.3% is completely wrong — actual rate is ~2.5%. The 21.3% figure would disqualify him from being a Biletnikoff Award winner.

### Jordyn Tyson (Arizona State)
**Status: ONE ERROR — injury history description is inaccurate**

- **College:** Arizona State — CONFIRMED (transferred from Colorado)
- **2026 Draft:** CONFIRMED, declared for draft, projected top-10 pick
- **Height 6'2":** CONFIRMED (measured 6'2.125" at combine)
- **Weight 200 lbs:** CLOSE — measured at **203 lbs** at combine; minor discrepancy
- **Projected top-10 pick:** CONFIRMED — Kiper had him No. 7; some post-combine boards have him lower (No. 24 in one PFF mock) due to not working out
- **Injury history (3 consecutive years):** PARTIALLY ACCURATE BUT INACCURATE COUNT — Tyson has been injured in multiple years but the framing is imprecise:
  - 2022 (at Colorado): ACL/MCL/PCL tear — missed entire 2023 season
  - 2024 (at Arizona State): Broken collarbone late in season
  - 2025 (at Arizona State): Hamstring injury, missed ~3 games; also did not work out at combine
  - His injuries span **4 seasons** (2022, 2023 recovery/missed, 2024, 2025), not simply "3 consecutive years." The injuries are real and a legitimate concern, but saying "3 consecutive years" mischaracterizes the timeline.

**Verdict:** Injury history claim is imprecise — injuries span 2022-2025 (four years), not exactly "3 consecutive years." Weight is 203 lbs, not 200 lbs (minor).

### Eli Stowers (Vanderbilt)
**Status: ONE ERROR — "any position" record claim is wrong**

- **College:** Vanderbilt — CONFIRMED (transferred from Texas A&M → New Mexico State → Vanderbilt)
- **2026 Draft:** CONFIRMED, declared for draft
- **Former QB:** CONFIRMED — was a four-star QB recruit at Texas A&M; was beaten out for the QB job at New Mexico State by Diego Pavia before converting to TE
- **45.5-inch vertical:** CONFIRMED — broke the combine **tight end** vertical jump record with a 45.5" vertical at the 2026 Combine
- **"Record for any position":** WRONG — the 45.5" is the **tight end position record**, not the all-position record. The all-time NFL Combine vertical jump record across all positions is **46 inches**, set by safety Gerald Sensabaugh in 2005. Stowers' mark is described as the second-highest vertical jump regardless of position since 2003, but it is not the overall record.

**Verdict:** The 45.5" vertical is real and is the tight end combine record. However, the claim it is a "record for any position" is factually wrong — Gerald Sensabaugh holds the all-position record at 46" (2005).

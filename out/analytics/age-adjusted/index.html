Age-Adjusted Dynasty Rankings

3-Year Outlook — player values adjusted for age trajectory

March 2026 · 40 Players · All 12 Rosters

“Raw dynasty values lie. A 32-year-old WR listed at 4,200 is not worth 4,200 to a dynasty manager with a 3-year horizon. I have adjusted every player for the cold arithmetic of age. The rankings that follow are what dynasty assets are actually worth to your future.”

~Love, Bimfle

Age Adjustment Model

Each player’s raw dynasty value is multiplied by an age-based coefficient reflecting how much of their productive output is expected over the next 3 years.

Ages 22–25

100%

Full Value

Ages 26–28

85%

Slight Decline

Ages 29–31

60%

Significant Decline

Ages 32+

25%

Terminal Value

Age coefficients apply to all positions. Position-specific aging notes are shown in the Position Age Curves section below.

Position Age Curves

Typical peak age window and decline cliff by position. Understanding these curves is the foundation of age-adjusted dynasty valuation.

QB

Peak

27–30

Cliff

34+

Age 20Age 40

QBs age the most gracefully. They peak later and hold value longer than any skill position. A 30-year-old QB is still a dynasty cornerstone. Career longevity means QBs are the lowest-risk age investment.

RB

Peak

23–26

Cliff

28+

Age 20Age 40

The most age-sensitive position in dynasty. The RB cliff arrives fast and hard — a 28-year-old RB is a liability unless your championship window is now. Age-adjusted value drops sharply for any RB over 27.

WR

Peak

24–28

Cliff

31+

Age 20Age 40

WRs have a wide prime window but dynasty market value starts declining before production does. Elite WRs can produce at 30–31, but 3-year outlook value dips significantly. Sell at 27, re-evaluate at 28.

TE

Peak

26–30

Cliff

33+

Age 20Age 40

TEs age the best of any skill position. Late development and long careers mean a 29-year-old TE can still have 4+ elite seasons ahead. Travis Kelce at 36 is the exception but TEs regularly produce through 32.

What is “Devy”?

Devy (short for “developmental”) refers to college football players you monitor for dynasty value before they declare for the NFL Draft. Some dynasty leagues allow a separate devy roster where managers can stash these players now and promote them to the main roster once drafted by an NFL team.

Tracking devy prospects gives dynasty managers a significant edge: securing elite talent before it hits the rookie draft market at premium cost. The earlier you identify the right players, the cheaper the acquisition.

BMFFFL Devy Rules

Current status: BMFFFL does not currently have a devy roster. All college players are tracked for informational purposes only — you cannot roster them yet.

If a devy roster is added, here's how it would work:

  • 3-spot devy roster — Managers can hold up to 3 college players at any time.
  • College players only — Players must be currently enrolled in college and not yet NFL Draft eligible.
  • Auto-promotion — Once a player declares for and is selected in the NFL Draft, they move to your main roster (or taxi squad).
  • Devy waiver wire — Free agent devy players would be available via a separate FAAB pool or waiver order.

Rule addition requires a majority vote at the Owners Meeting. Bring it up at the 2026 annual meeting.

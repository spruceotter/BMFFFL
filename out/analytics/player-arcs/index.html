Position Aging Curves

Typical peak age windows per position — the foundation of dynasty roster construction

QBPeak: 27–32
Decline starts

Age 33

Longest arc in football. QBs hit their peak later and hold it longer. Dynasty QBs remain elite into their early 30s.

RBPeak: 23–25
Decline starts

Age 27

Shortest window of any skill position. RBs decline sharply at 27-28. Buy young, sell at peak — no exceptions.

WRPeak: 24–28
Decline starts

Age 29

Medium-length arc. Route runners age better than speed merchants. Sell window is 28-29, not 26-27 like RBs.

TEPeak: 25–29
Decline starts

Age 30

TE development is slow — most top TEs hit their prime in year 3-4. The decline is also the most gradual of any position.

Career Arc Patterns

The four fundamental career trajectories in dynasty fantasy football

The Ascent

BUY NOW
Bijan RobinsonBrock Bowers

Young rising player entering their prime window. Dynasty value climbing steeply. Buy aggressively — you are acquiring peak seasons at a discount.

The Dynasty Peak

HOLD FIRMLY
Justin JeffersonLamar JacksonJosh Allen

Established star at maximum dynasty value. Production is elite and consistent. Hold tightly — this is what you built your roster around.

The Graceful Decline

SELL / CUT
Davante AdamsAlvin KamaraTravis Kelce

Aging veteran past their peak. Production remains decent but dynasty value is eroding season by season. The sell window has passed for most; extract what you can.

The Second Wind

SELL INTO BUZZ
Derrick Henry (2024)

The rare aging-curve exception. An older player defies expectations with a late-career resurgence. Context-specific: Henry's 2024 was historic. Do not build a dynasty strategy around it.

"The career arc is the dynasty manager's most critical analytical framework. One acquires players on the ascent and sells them at the peak. Those who hold through the decline are sentenced to mediocrity."

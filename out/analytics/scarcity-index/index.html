Scarcity-Adjusted Trade Value

Premiums above standard dynasty value charts

TE Premium

+25%

TE scarcity at 91/100 is extreme. In trade negotiations, a locked-in TE1 commands a 25% markup above standard dynasty value charts. Acquiring Brock Bowers or Colston Loveland costs more than the chart says.

RB Premium

+15%

RB scarcity at 82/100 adds a 15% premium on true bell cows. Backfield committees are everywhere — teams will overpay to secure a workhorse. Volume backs are worth more than they appear on paper.

Medium scarcity (61/100) creates a modest WR1 premium. Top receivers are fine; the scarcity is at WR2 depth. Teams should target WR2/WR3 upside in the rookie draft rather than chasing elite WR1 prices.

QB Premium

No premium

Low QB scarcity (35/100) means no premium above standard value. The SF market is saturated with viable starters. Do not overpay for a second QB — the position restocks easily via rookie drafts and waiver wire.

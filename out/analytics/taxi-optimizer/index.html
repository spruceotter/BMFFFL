Keeper Rules Reference

3

3 keepers max per team

Each manager may designate up to 3 players to keep for the upcoming season.

↑

Cost: 1 round earlier than drafted

A player drafted in Round 4 costs a Round 3 pick to keep. The pick used equals the round above where they were drafted.

⊘

Waiver additions cannot be kept

You may only keep players you drafted. Players picked up off waivers or the free-agent pool are not keeper-eligible.

0

Minimum: 0 keepers

Keeping is always optional. You may choose to keep nobody and enter the draft with a full slate of picks.

Bimfle’s Taxi Strategy Guide

  • 1.

    Promote when the landing spot is locked

    If a rookie has secured a clear starting role by OTAs, promote immediately. Don't gamble on a roster-spot claim during the season.

  • 2.

    Hold through at least half a season before cutting

    Second-year players often need 8 weeks of live NFL action before their role crystalizes. Cutting in May based on depth charts alone is premature.

  • 3.

    The cost of cutting is an open taxi spot, not a loss

    Cutting a taxi player doesn't cost a pick — it frees a spot for the next rookie class. Think of it as reclaiming a future asset, not discarding a current one.

  • 4.

    Taxi spots are currency in rookie drafts

    The best teams entering the 2026 draft have open taxi slots and high picks. A full, stale taxi squad heading into the draft is a liability.

  • 5.

    Never taxi a player past their eligibility window

    A player hitting Year 3 who has not proven fantasy value is a roster clog. Promote only if you intend to start them. Cut otherwise — the taxi is not a storage unit.

“The taxi squad is the dynasty manager’s junior varsity. Promote the stars, hold the maybes, and cut the depth chart fodder before it costs you a roster spot that a real prospect deserves.”

~ Love, Bimfle

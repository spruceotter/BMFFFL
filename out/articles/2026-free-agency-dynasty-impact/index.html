2026 NFL Free Agency: Dynasty Impact Report

The 2026 free agency period has been one of the most active in recent memory — QB reshuffles, elite RBs switching teams, and several WR/TE moves that reshape dynasty values. Here's what you need to know before the market prices it all in.

Timing note: Free agency opened March 12, 2026. The NFL Draft runs April 24-26. Values are in motion until landing spots are final. Treat all signals below as directional — confirm before trading.

Quarterback Moves

Kyler Murray to Minnesota — More Noise Than Signal

Murray signed with Minnesota after Arizona released him. On paper, Kevin O'Connell's offense alongside Justin Jefferson, Jordan Addison, and T.J. Hockenson is an enormous environment upgrade. The reality: Murray's production has declined six consecutive seasons — from QB3 to QB20 — and he's coming off a foot injury.

His rushing floor keeps him relevant in Superflex. But dynasty managers should not overreact to the scheme fit. The real casualty is J.J. McCarthy, who drops to undraftable in redraft and QB31 in dynasty. Justin Jefferson's ceiling rises modestly with a more mobile QB, but the Murray–Jefferson connection is unproven.

BMFFFL context: In a Superflex league, Murray is still rostered for his dual-threat floor. His value as a trade piece rises slightly based on the Jefferson halo — but the production trend doesn't support buying at an inflated price.

Malik Willis to Miami — Caution on Dolphins Pass Catchers

Willis signed three years, $67.5M ($45M guaranteed) — closing the Tua Tagovailoa era in Miami. Willis averaged only 18 pass attempts per game in his limited starts, which is a meaningful ceiling on Jaylen Waddle's and Tyreek Hill's target volume.

BMFFFL context: Waddle and Hill owners should treat this as a mild negative until volume proves out in 2026. Do not buy Hill or Waddle based on their pre-Willis values. Tua landed in Atlanta on a bridge deal; his dynasty value is minimal.

Running Back Moves

Kenneth Walker III to the Chiefs — Buy

The clearest buy signal of the entire free agency period. Walker signed three years, $43.05M base (max $45M, $28.7M guaranteed) with Kansas City, bringing a 14.7% explosive run rate — second in the NFL in 2025 — to a Patrick Mahomes offense that has badly needed a backfield weapon.

Chiefs RBs finished second-worst in yards per carry the last two seasons. Andy Reid gets a three-down back with genuine pass-catching ability. Walker moves into the RB1 conversation in dynasty. His Superflex value gets a secondary boost from the Mahomes passing game volume he'll absorb.

This is a buy now. The market will price this in quickly once training camp confirms the role.

Breece Hall (Franchise Tagged) — Hold, Do Not Sell

The Jets applied the RB franchise tag to Hall ($14.3M) rather than extend him — signaling possible trade discussions. Hall is 24, has elite athleticism, and produced 1,065 rushing yards in a broken offense in 2025. His dynasty ceiling is top-five if he lands on a functional offense.

Do not sell low. A trade to a contending team makes Hall a top-five dynasty RB again. The tag is actually a buy signal in disguise — it keeps him available for a trade to a better situation at the deadline.

Travis Etienne Jr. to New Orleans — Hold (Eyes Open)

Etienne signed four years, $52M in New Orleans coming off a 1,399-scrimmage-yard season. The concern: Saints RBs ranked last in PPR points in 2025, and the offense remains a rebuilding project. Etienne's talent is legitimate; his environment is not. He's a hold with buy-upside if New Orleans modernizes their scheme, and a sell if the Saints offense remains stagnant.

Wide Receiver Moves

DJ Moore to Buffalo — Buy

The Bears traded Moore (plus their own 5th-round pick) to Buffalo for a 2026 second-round pick. Moore has logged four seasons of 1,100+ receiving yards while cycling through a parade of mediocre quarterbacks (Fields, Williams, Newton, and others). He now plays alongside Josh Allen — arguably the best passer of his career.

At 29, Moore's dynasty window isn't infinite. But a 1,200-yard season in Year 1 with Allen is a realistic outcome. He's a 2026 redraft WR2 with dynasty value as a bridge asset. Buy at current prices before the market adjusts.

Michael Pittman Jr. to Pittsburgh — Monitor

Pittman was traded from Indianapolis to Pittsburgh at age 27. The Steelers desperately need a receiving option as they search for a long-term QB — likely a 2026 draft pick. Pittman has renewed upside as a WR1 in a rebuilding offense. The variable is QB quality; track the Steelers' draft activity closely.

Mike Evans to San Francisco — Sell (Dynasty)

Evans signed three years, $60.4M at age 33. The 49ers lack proven weapons beyond Ricky Pearsall with Kittle recovering, and Shanahan's system should produce red-zone targets. The risk: Evans turns 33 during the season, and the dynasty window is short. Sell now if you own him, or draft him only in redraft as a WR2/3.

Tight End Notes

Isaiah Likely to the Giants — Buy-Low (TE)

Likely signed three years, $40M with New York — establishing him as the primary receiving TE in New York for the first time in his career. He showed genuine pass-catching ability as Kelce's backup in Baltimore; the Giants give him the starting role. He's a dynasty TE2 sleeper at current prices.

Travis Kelce Re-Signs with Kansas City

One year, up to $15M. Removes retirement uncertainty. His dynasty value remains TE1-adjacent for 2026 only — his value at the end of the season depends on whether he retires. Do not build a multi-year dynasty plan around Kelce.

Kyle Pitts Franchise Tagged in Atlanta

The Falcons applied the TE franchise tag to Pitts (~$16.3M), keeping him with Kevin Stefanski's offense alongside Bijan Robinson and Drake London. The tag signals the Falcons view him as a long-term piece. Hold Pitts. With Tua Tagovailoa added as QB depth behind Michael Penix Jr., the Atlanta offense has more competent QB options than it has in years.

BMFFFL-Specific Context

Superflex format implications: QB moves have downstream effects on WR/RB/TE values. Murray to Minnesota raises Jefferson's ceiling (mild buy). Willis to Miami is neutral-to-negative for Hill and Waddle. The Geno Smith–Jets pairing provides floor for Breece Hall and Garrett Wilson without raising their ceilings.

4-point passing TD scoring: BMFFFL's 4pt passing TD structure (vs. 6pt in some leagues) slightly depresses QB values relative to skill positions. This means Kenneth Walker's move to Kansas City is even more valuable than in a standard league — he absorbs passing game volume in a way that translates fully to PPR scoring.

The March window: The best time to act on these moves is before the NFL Draft (April 24-26) reshapes the picture again. Landing spots for 2026 rookies will reset dozens of dynasty values at once. Move on the signals above before draft buzz creates a second wave of market volatility.

2026 NFL Draft: Dynasty Impact Report

By Flint, BMFFFL Director of Operations

Published: April 24, 2026 — Night of the Draft

The Night That Broke the Mock Drafts

Round 1 of the 2026 NFL Draft is in the books, and if you had the consensus mocks memorized, you got punished. Picks 3 through 17 went to teams nobody expected. Carnell Tate landed in Tennessee instead of New York. Jeremiyah Love went to Arizona instead of Tennessee. Ty Simpson — projected as a Day 2 quarterback by most analysts — was taken by the Rams at #13 in what might be the shocking pick of the decade.

Every BMFFFL roster is affected. Some teams just got a massive dynasty gift. Others watched a key investment erode. Here's the full breakdown — team by team.

Team-by-Team Impact

🔴 El Rioux Grandes — The Jackpot Team

Record: 4-10 | Rookie Pick: ~1st overall

El Rioux Grandes finished dead last in 2025. In a dynasty league, last place is actually a gift — and the 2026 draft delivers one of the strongest rookie classes in years.

The situation: Grandes' RB corps is a collection of aging vets and depth pieces. Patrick Mahomes (age 30) is their franchise QB — a great asset, but they don't need another QB.

The verdict: Take Jeremiyah Love at #1 overall. Love is the most complete dynasty prospect since Bijan Robinson. He heads to Arizona in an offense built around Marvin Harrison Jr. (the best young receiver in football), giving him favorable box numbers and a franchise that's committed to offensive investment. Love at #1 is not a question — it's a destiny.

Impact on existing roster: Positive across the board. The entire Grandes rebuild accelerates.

🟡 Whale Tails — The Steady Contender

Record: 10-4 | Rookie Pick: ~11th overall

Tubes94's Whale Tails were the 2025 runners-up. They have Bijan Robinson (ATL, age 24) and Breece Hall (NYJ, age 24) as their elite RBs, plus Jordan Love, Trevor Lawrence, and Kyle Pitts on offense.

The situation: Strong but aging WR corps. Puka Nacua (LAR, 24) is their best young WR. They need WR1-caliber youth.

The verdict: Drafting late, Whale Tails likely picks somewhere in the range of Jordyn Tyson (NO, WR) or KC Concepcion (CLE, WR) — solid depth adds but not franchise-changers.

Note on Breece Hall: Hall plays for the Jets, who just drafted three players (Bailey EDGE, Sadiq TE, Cooper Jr. WR) in Round 1. The Jets' improving roster is modestly positive for Hall's dynasty value.

🔵 SexMachineAndyD — The Contender with a Dynasty Heart

Record: 9-5 | Rookie Pick: ~9th overall

SexMachineAndyD has the most balanced dynasty roster in BMFFFL: Brock Bowers (TE, age 23), Malik Nabers (WR, age 22, on IR), Jaxon Smith-Njigba (SEA, age 24), and De'Von Achane (MIA, age 24). A powerhouse in the making.

The situation: They're a contender now and in the future. Late picks mean they're picking from leftovers after the top 8 prospects are gone.

The Ty Simpson angle: SexMachineAndyD owns Kyren Williams (LAR, RB, age 25) and Jordan Whittington (LAR, WR, age 25). Ty Simpson joining the Rams at #13 is a tangible positive — McVay has historically elevated every QB he's worked with. Simpson running McVay's system means Williams and Whittington get a legitimate aerial attack to work beside. Don't sleep on this.

The Jadarian Price note: Price (SEA, RB) goes to Seattle, where SexMachineAndyD's JSN plays. A competent running game in Seattle is a minor boost for JSN's targets. Minimal impact, but directionally positive.

🟣 THE Shameful Saggy Sack — The Champions

Record: 8-6 | Rookie Pick: ~12th overall (champion picks last)

tdtd19844 won the 2025 championship — and in dynasty, winning comes with a cost: the last rookie pick. Their roster is deep in youth (Caleb Williams CHI QB, Brian Thomas WR, Rashee Rice WR, DJ Giddens RB) but not loaded with superstars.

The situation: Picks last, so they're taking the 12th-best available prospect. In a strong class this still yields value.

The verdict: The 2026 R1 class is deep enough that pick #12 likely yields a B-tier prospect — perhaps a Day 1 WR from rounds 2-3 of the NFL Draft, or a rotational RB with upside. The champion's challenge is real, but the class compensates.

🟢 Juicy Bussy — The A.J. Brown Wildcard

Record: 7-7 | Rookie Pick: ~8th overall

delaurmr's Juicy Bussy is sitting on a dynasty sleeper play that nobody else in the league has: they own A.J. Brown (PHI, age 28). And on draft night, the Eagles traded up to take Makai Lemon (WR, USC) at #20.

Let that sink in. Brown is the reigning WR1 in Philadelphia. He has 2-3 more elite years ahead of him. And his eventual replacement just landed in his building.

The play: If Juicy Bussy drafts Lemon in the rookie draft (likely around pick 8 in a 12-team league, assuming the top 7 go before her), they own the Eagles' WR situation for the next decade. Brown produces now. Lemon takes over when Brown steps aside. It's not often you can own a dynasty WR room this clearly. This is the most compelling duo in the 2026 BMFFFL off-season.

Current roster depth: Their WRs (Amon-Ra St. Brown, Tee Higgins, George Pickens, Nico Collins) are all veterans 25-27. Lemon would be the youngest WR on the roster by 3 years — and have the highest dynasty ceiling.

🟠 Showtyme Boyz — The Jalen Hurts Machine

Record: 6-8 | Rookie Pick: ~7th overall

maleskcr1 fields a roster built around Jalen Hurts (PHI, age 27) in a superflex setup — and Hurts is already the most valuable dynasty QB in the league. They also have Travis Hunter (JAX, WR/CB, age 22) and Drake Maye (NE, QB, age 23) as young cornerstones.

The situation: Drafts in the middle of the pack. Could target the best available WR or a Day 2 QB with upside.

The Will Levis note: Levis is on their roster (TEN, age 26, on IR). With Tate now in Tennessee, Levis' time is probably numbered — Tate's arrival signals a rebuild in Nashville. Showtyme Boyz should consider moving Levis while there's still value to extract.

🔵 Franks Little Beauties — The Dynasty Core

Record: 6-8 | Rookie Pick: ~6th overall

eldridm20 has the youngest average roster age in BMFFFL. Josh Allen (BUF, age 29), Ja'Marr Chase (CIN, age 26), Brandon Aiyuk (SF, age 28) — all franchise players. And they're loaded with young depth.

The situation: They need one more elite piece to become a true dynasty powerhouse. With the 6th pick in the rookie draft, they're right at the edge of the premium prospects.

The call: If Love goes #1 and Tate/Mendoza go 2-3, the 6th pick likely nets Makai Lemon OR Jadarian Price — both solid values. Lemon pairs beautifully with Aiyuk in a WR-rich stable. Price (SEA) gives them a young RB next to TreVeyon Henderson (NE).

🟡 Booty Cheeks — The Cardinals Cluster

Record: 6-8 | Rookie Pick: ~5th overall

Note: Booty Cheeks is currently an orphaned roster — former manager Escuelas (MCSchools) departed the league in 2026. The roster is stewarded as TBD - Orphan Roster pending new owner placement.

The Booty Cheeks roster features Ashton Jeanty (LV, RB, age 22) and Tetairoa McMillan (CAR, WR, age 23) — last year's top two overall picks. They also have Trey Benson (ARI, RB, age 23) and Michael Wilson (ARI, WR, age 26) in the Cardinals backfield.

The big news: Jeremiyah Love just joined Arizona at #3 overall. That's the same backfield where Benson and Wilson play.

The Benson question: Love's arrival almost certainly reduces Trey Benson's dynasty value. Benson was the ARI RB1 heir apparent — now he's the #2 at best. Booty Cheeks should monitor this situation carefully. The silver lining: Love's presence makes the ARI offense better overall, which helps Wilson's receiving numbers.

Picking 5th: Booty Cheeks is well-positioned to grab a premium WR. Makai Lemon or Carnell Tate could still be available here if the first four picks go RB-heavy.

🔴 eldridsm — The Cam Ward/TEN Long Game

Record: 5-9 | Rookie Pick: ~4th overall

eldridsm's roster is a chaotic mix of aging stars and young depth. Cam Ward (TEN, QB, age 23) is their franchise QB — the 2025 #1 overall pick playing in Tennessee.

The seismic development: Tennessee just drafted Carnell Tate (WR) at #4 and Keldric Faulk (EDGE) at #31. Tate is a precision route runner who immediately upgrades Cam Ward's target options. This is directly positive for eldridsm — Ward now has a legitimate WR1 to throw to in his second season. Don't underestimate how much this matters for Ward's dynasty value.

The pick: With the 4th selection, eldridsm is in premium territory. In superflex, Fernando Mendoza might be the call if no team has taken him earlier. In a standard format, Makai Lemon or Ty Simpson could be the target.

🟢 Really Big Rings — The Double Threat

Record: 5-9 | Rookie Pick: ~3rd overall

rbr's Really Big Rings is a curious roster. Justin Jefferson (MIN, WR, age 26) is one of the best dynasty WRs alive. But beyond Jefferson, they have two young players at positions that just got directly threatened by the 2026 draft.

Threat #1 — Elic Ayomanor (TEN, WR, age 22): Carnell Tate went to Tennessee at #4. Tate is a higher-graded prospect who will immediately compete for (and likely win) the WR1 role in Nashville. Ayomanor, who was developing as the TEN WR1, just got passed on the depth chart.

Threat #2 — Zach Charbonnet (SEA, RB, age 25): Jadarian Price went to Seattle at #32. Price is a first-round investment — teams rarely take RBs in R1 just to make them backups. Charbonnet's dynasty value is diminished.

The irony: The 3rd pick in the rookie draft could bring rbr Carnell Tate — the exact player who is devaluing their Ayomanor. Do they take Tate (the better dynasty piece, but an awkward double-TEN investment) and cut/trade Ayomanor? Or do they let Tate fall and double down on Ayomanor's development? This is one of the most interesting dynasty decisions of the off-season.

The verdict: Take Tate. Sell Ayomanor while you still can.

🟡 Earn it — The NYJ TE Conflict

Record: 5-9 | Rookie Pick: ~2nd overall

Cogdeill11's Earn it has one of the youngest and deepest rosters in BMFFFL. Jahmyr Gibbs (DET, RB, age 24) anchors an offense with J.J. McCarthy (MIN, QB, age 23), Michael Penix (ATL, QB, age 25), Omarion Hampton (LAC, RB, age 23), and Colston Loveland (CHI, TE, age 22).

The conflict: Earn it owns Mason Taylor (NYJ, TE, age 21) — a promising young tight end with a clear path to a starting role in New York. But the Jets just drafted Kenyon Sadiq at #16. Sadiq is a higher-graded TE prospect landing in the exact situation where Mason Taylor was building value.

The fork: Pick #2 could go one of two ways:

  1. Take Sadiq directly — this "blocks" Taylor by consolidating the Jets TE situation, accepting reduced dynasty value from Taylor while gaining Sadiq.
  2. Take Carnell Tate (WR) or Fernando Mendoza (QB in superflex) — maximize the pick value, accept that Taylor's dynasty value has dropped.

The mathematically correct play is almost certainly the WR/QB route — Tate or Mendoza are better dynasty value than Sadiq at pick #2. But the Taylor situation needs a decision either way.

The verdict: Take Tate at #2 (or Mendoza in superflex). Then evaluate whether to move Mason Taylor or hold him alongside Sadiq.

🔵 Schoolcraft Football Team — The Dynasty Juggernaut

Record: 13-1 | Rookie Pick: ~10th overall

MLSchools12's Schoolcraft Football Team went 13-1 in 2025. They don't need rookies — they need their existing pieces to stay healthy. Lamar Jackson (BAL, age 29), CeeDee Lamb (DAL, age 27), Jordan Addison (MIN, age 24), Jaylen Waddle (DEN, age 27), Bucky Irving (TB, age 23).

The situation: Late pick, loaded roster. They'll take best available value — likely a Day 2 WR sleeper or a developmental QB. Nothing transformative expected.

Note on Davante Adams: Adams (LAR, age 33) is on their roster and likely entering his final years. Ty Simpson arriving in Los Angeles doesn't make Adams younger, but it does mean Adams' final season(s) will be in an offense with real QB infrastructure.

Key Storylines to Watch

1. The Juicy Bussy AJ Brown → Makai Lemon handoff. This is the most compelling dynasty play in BMFFFL right now. If Juicy Bussy can secure Lemon, they own the Eagles' WR room for the next 5-7 years. Brown produces now; Lemon inherits. Watch this pick closely.

2. Really Big Rings' Tennessee problem. Carnell Tate directly threatens Elic Ayomanor. Jadarian Price directly threatens Zach Charbonnet. One team, two threats. The correct play is to take Tate at #3 and trade Ayomanor at peak value.

3. The Ty Simpson Rams effect. Nobody had Simpson going to McVay at #13. If he develops, Kyren Williams and Jordan Whittington (both owned by SexMachineAndyD) are the beneficiaries. McVay hasn't failed a QB yet — this is quietly a big deal for SexMachineAndyD's long-term outlook.

4. Earn it's Mason Taylor decision. The Jets drafting Sadiq doesn't mean Taylor is worthless — but Sadiq being higher-graded means Taylor's upside is capped. Earn it should decide now: commit to Taylor, or move him while a buyer still sees potential.

5. Cam Ward just got a real WR1. eldridsm's Cam Ward (TEN) spent his rookie season in an offense without a true WR1. Carnell Tate's arrival changes the math completely. Ward-to-Tate might be the most important dynasty connection entering 2026.

BMFFFL Rookie Draft 2026: The Complete Guide

The 2026 BMFFFL Rookie Draft takes place on the first Friday of June. It's a 4-round linear draft — 48 total picks across 12 teams. What happens in those 48 selections will shape the BMFFFL for the next half-decade.

Here's everything you need to prepare.

The 2026 NFL Draft Class: Overview

The 2026 class enters an NFL landscape that has evolved significantly toward pass-heavy offenses. This benefits WRs and TEs over RBs in the long run, but the 2026 RB class led by Jeremiyah Love provides genuine first-round talent regardless.

Class summary:

  • RB: Strong at the top (Love is elite), thin in the middle
  • WR: Deep class with multiple first-round options (Williams, Lemon, plus depth)
  • TE: Best TE class in several years (Sadiq, Stowers — both top-10 dynasty)
  • QB: Thin — Will Howard is the clear top option but profiles as a multi-year developer

Prospect Rankings

Tier 1: Can't Miss

1. Jeremiyah Love — RB, Notre Dame The unanimous 1.01 in 2026 dynasty drafts. Love ran for 1,400+ yards and 18 TDs in his final college season, earning first-team All-American honors. His combination of speed (4.38 40), vision, and receiving ability makes him a complete back. Dynasty ADP: 1.01.

Landing spot matters: Love needs a run-first offense to hit his ceiling. Teams like Baltimore, Tampa Bay, or Tennessee would be ideal. An Air Raid team would suppress his value.

2. Ryan Williams — WR, Alabama The best WR prospect since the elite 2022 class. Williams is a contested-catch specialist with elite size (6'2", 210 lbs) and exceptional separation on intermediate routes. 1,200+ receiving yards in 2025. Dynasty ADP: 1.02-1.03.

3. Makai Lemon — WR, USC The 2025 Biletnikoff Award winner as the nation's best receiver. Lemon is a route-running technician rather than an athletic freak, but his production was among the best in the country. Dynasty ADP: 1.03-1.05.

Tier 2: High-Upside Early Round

4. Kenyon Sadiq — TE, Oregon The consensus TE1 in a deep TE class. Sadiq is a complete pass-catcher — excellent hands, good route runner, above-average blocker. In dynasty Superflex, TE1 of a class goes earlier than in non-SF leagues. Dynasty ADP: 1.04-1.06.

5. TreVeyon Henderson — RB, Ohio State Henderson is the higher-upside backup RB in this class. Excellent athleticism, good hands as a receiver out of the backfield. Some durability questions from college, but the talent is first-round. Dynasty ADP: 1.05-1.07.

6. Eli Stowers — TE, Vanderbilt The 2025 Senior Bowl and combine darling. His 45.5" vertical set a combine record for tight ends. The athleticism is elite; the production (68/912/9) backs it up. Dynasty ADP: 1.06-1.09.

Tier 3: Proven Upside, Situational Risk

7-12: Second-wave WRs and RBs This tier includes a mix of proven college producers and developmental prospects whose dynasty value hinges heavily on NFL landing spots. Names to watch: Emeka Egbuka (WR, Ohio State — if reclassified), Evan Stewart (WR), and several RBs with workhorse potential.

Will Howard — QB, Ohio State The top QB of the 2026 class, Howard is a pro-ready passer with good arm strength and solid decision-making. In Superflex, a starting-caliber rookie QB is a major asset. Dynasty ADP: late round 1 / early round 2 depending on team needs.

Linear Format Explained

In a linear draft, you pick at the same position in every round:

  • Cmaleski: picks 1.01, 2.01, 3.01, 4.01
  • Escuelas: picks 1.02, 2.02, 3.02, 4.02
  • etc.

This rewards teams that finish last — they get four picks in a row at the top of their respective rounds. One bad season in dynasty is a real opportunity if you use the picks well.

Team-by-Team Needs

Cmaleski (1.01, 2.01, 3.01, 4.01)

Holding the best pick in the draft, Cmaleski can select the top player regardless of position. With Jeremiyah Love at 1.01, they get an elite RB. Their roster depth suggests flexibility in rounds 2-4 — a TE target (Stowers range) at 2.01 would be excellent.

Escuelas (1.02, 2.02, 3.02, 4.02)

After 4 seasons with 0 playoff appearances (15-41 career record), Escuelas is in full rebuild mode with four picks in the top-2 of each round. Ryan Williams or Kenyon Sadiq at 1.02 are both excellent outcomes. Multiple picks give them the most rebuilding leverage of any team in this draft.

MLSchools12

Picking late in the first round. They're already elite — their picks are value rather than need. The aging TE situation (Hunter Henry, 31) makes Sadiq or Stowers a legitimate consideration even late in round 1.

tdtd19844

Defending champion picking in the middle of the first round. They need to reload around their championship core — fresh rookie talent fits their 3-year window.

Draft Strategy by Team Type

Win-Now Teams (2-4 year window)

Teams like MLSchools12 and Tubes94 should be prioritizing players who can contribute in 2027-2028, not players who need 3-5 years to develop.

Target: Kenyon Sadiq (immediate TE1), veteran-adjacent WRs with clear pathways, RBs in strong landing spots.

Avoid: High-ceiling, low-floor developmental QBs who won't start for 3 years.

Rebuilding Teams

Teams like Escuelas and Cogdeill11 should be accumulating the highest-upside players regardless of timeline.

Target: Jeremiyah Love (elite talent deserves elite draft capital), Will Howard (Superflex QB at a discount), multiple picks across rounds 2-4.

Avoid: Safe, floor-heavy players who are fine but won't win you a championship.

Middle-of-the-Pack Teams

The most common mistake in dynasty is the middle-of-the-pack team that splits the difference — not quite good enough to win now, not committed enough to rebuild.

Strategy: Pick a lane. Either add a piece that pushes you into contention this year, or be willing to go young and set up for 2028.

6 Seasons of Data: What We've Learned About Winning in BMFFFL

Six seasons. 83 regular-season weeks. 257 trades. Five unique champions (six trophies). Here's what the data says about how to win the BMFFFL.

The Champions, At a Glance

Pattern 1: In 6 seasons, only 2 champions entered the playoffs seeded 1st or 2nd. The other 4 were seeded 3rd or lower.

Pattern 2: The 1-seed has won exactly once (MLSchools12 in 2021). The 4-seed has won twice (Grandes 2022, tdtd19844 2025).

Pattern 3: rbr was runner-up two consecutive years (2021, 2022) without ever winning a championship.

The Dominance of MLSchools12

No statistical analysis of the BMFFFL is complete without acknowledging the outlier at the top.

MLSchools12 career stats:

  • All-time record: 68-15 (.819 win percentage)
  • Championships: 2 (2021, 2024)
  • Playoff appearances: 6 out of 6 (100%)
  • Two 13-1 regular seasons (2023, 2025) — without a championship
  • Semifinal exits: 2 (2023, 2025)

The 13-1 finishes without championships are the most fascinating data point. MLSchools12 has been the single-most dominant regular-season team in BMFFFL history yet has a 2/4 playoff win rate when seeded 1st.

Lesson: Regular-season excellence does not guarantee championships in dynasty. A 6-team playoff with single-elimination matchups introduces enough variance that even the best roster loses.

The anchor: Bijan Robinson, selected in the 2022 or 2023 rookie draft when he was still a college prospect, became one of the best dynasty RBs in the BMFFFL.

Lesson: Tanking is a valid strategy. Three years of pain for one championship is a good trade.

The Cost of Staying in the Middle

Some BMFFFL owners have hovered near .500 for most of league history — competitive enough to avoid the best draft picks, not good enough to win championships.

This "middle class" is the worst place to be in dynasty:

  • You miss out on top rookie picks (those go to bad teams)
  • You're not winning championships
  • Your window closes as your core ages

eldridsm (41-42, .494 career, 1 runner-up in 2020) is the clearest example. Competitive for most of the league's history but stuck without a championship or a path to one.

Tubes94 (34-36, .486, runner-up 2025) may be at an inflection point — their young core of Trevor Lawrence and Bijan Robinson gives them a legitimate path to a championship.

Lesson: Be decisive. Either compete fully now or rebuild fully for later. The middle ground compounds over time into irrelevance.

The 257-Trade League

BMFFFL has averaged 42.8 trades per season (257 total over 6 seasons). This is significantly above average for a dynasty league and reflects an active, engaged ownership group.

What the trade activity signals:

  • Owners are willing to reshape their rosters
  • Pick speculation is common (teams trade future picks speculatively)
  • The market is generally efficient — you rarely steal a trade

The most famous trade in BMFFFL history isn't recorded in a single transaction — it's the series of acquisitions that built MLSchools12's roster around Christian McCaffrey, Lamar Jackson, and CeeDee Lamb. Each piece came from a different deal.

Lesson: Dynasty success isn't built in the draft alone. It's built through active portfolio management. The best BMFFFL managers are constantly asking: "Am I getting full value from this player?"

The Seed 4 Phenomenon

Two of six BMFFFL championships were won by the 4-seed. This isn't random noise — it's a structural feature of the format.

In a 3-week single-elimination playoff (weeks 15, 16, 17):

  • Three matchups worth of variance is enough to swing outcomes significantly
  • The best team in the league has roughly a 25-30% chance of winning the championship
  • The 4th, 5th, and 6th seeds collectively have a meaningful chance

Grandes 2022: Won as 4th seed in a season where three teams finished 10-4. Survived the semifinals over MLSchools12 by 2.80 points.

tdtd19844 2025: Won as 4th seed with an 8-6 regular season record, beating the field in three playoff weeks.

Lesson: Making the playoffs is the goal. Once you're in, seed matters less than matchup variance.

2020 champion. Five consecutive playoff misses since. Career record now 38-45 (.458) — below .500 for the first time.

The 2020 championship was built around the right players at the right time. Alvin Kamara's Christmas Day explosion sealed the title. But the core aged and wasn't replaced.

Lesson: The year you win the championship, start planning for the rebuild. Cogdeill11's post-2020 history shows what happens when you don't.

Key Conclusions

  1. Regular-season dominance ≠ championships (MLSchools12's two 13-1 exits prove this)
  2. Rebuilding works (tdtd19844 from 3-11 to champion in 3 years)
  3. The middle class is a trap (near-.500 without a path to a top pick or a championship)
  4. QB depth compounds (Superflex means every QB matters)
  5. The trade market is active — use it to reshape your roster
  6. Get into the playoffs — seed is secondary to simply qualifying

The 2026 BMFFFL season begins in September. Based on six seasons of data, the likely contenders are the teams with the youngest cores, the deepest QB rooms, and the flexibility to adapt. MLSchools12 is still the standard. Everyone else is hunting them.

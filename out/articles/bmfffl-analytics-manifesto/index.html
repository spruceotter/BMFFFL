The BMFFFL Analytics Manifesto

Six seasons of BMFFFL data have produced a few hard-won lessons about how to think about dynasty football. This isn't a rankings piece. It's a framework — a set of principles that have proven durable across 2020, 2021, 2022, 2023, 2024, and 2025.

Principle 1: Dynasty Is Not a Season — It's a Portfolio

The BMFFFL is a 13-game regular season followed by a 6-team playoff. Optimizing for one season is not the same as optimizing for dynasty success.

The evidence: Cogdeill11 won in 2020 riding Alvin Kamara's historic Christmas Day performance. Then declined steadily — from 10-3 in 2020 to 4-9 in 2025. A championship built around an aging RB and a run-first approach does not compound.

Contrast with MLSchools12: 68-15 all-time (.819 win %), two championships (2021, 2024), the only owner to make the playoffs in every single season. Their roster philosophy has always been youth at skill positions with elite QB play. They reload without rebuilding.

The lesson: treat your roster like a portfolio of assets. Diversify across age, position, and NFL context. Don't sell futures to chase a pennant.

Principle 2: RB Is the Riskiest Asset Class

Running backs produce the most fantasy points and are also the fastest-declining assets in dynasty. In a league with Bijan Robinson and Christian McCaffrey on rosters, RB value is stratified: the top 3-4 are irreplaceable, the rest are volatile.

Look at the 2025 season: Bucky Irving was a key piece for MLSchools12. Two years ago he didn't have a roster spot in BMFFFL. Josh Jacobs — Green Bay's bell cow at age 28 — was available for a mid-round pick in 2022.

Dynasty RB valuation principle: the premium is for early-20s RBs with workhorse roles. Pay for 22-year-old bell cows. Be cautious with 28-year-olds regardless of current production.

The one exception: in Superflex leagues like BMFFFL, elite QBs hold value longer. Lamar Jackson at 29 is still worth a first-round pick. Christian McCaffrey at 29 is not.

Principle 3: The Superflex Tax Is Real

BMFFFL runs Superflex (SF) — you can start a second QB in your flex. This changes everything.

In a non-SF league, the QB market has a top-3 elite tier and a deep serviceable tier. In SF, every QB above a replacement level is scarce. You need two startable QBs.

The implication: rookie QBs are the highest-ceiling picks in a Superflex dynasty draft. A 22-year-old QB like Lamar Jackson (drafted in the first round of the 2020 BMFFFL startup) compounds in value over a decade. No other position ages as gracefully in dynasty.

BMFFFL Superflex lesson: if you can acquire a young elite QB at a discount because of short-term concerns (college system fit, weapons, coaching), do it. The opportunity cost of missing on a 23-year-old franchise QB is enormous.

Principle 4: Playoff Seed Matters Less Than You Think

The two 4th-seed championships in BMFFFL history (Grandes 2022, tdtd19844 2025) are not outliers — they're the expected outcome of a high-variance 3-week playoff format.

In a 6-team playoff, the 1-seed is only marginally more likely to win the championship than the 4-seed, because:

  1. Three games of variance is huge
  2. Home field doesn't exist in fantasy
  3. The best regular-season team is not always the hottest team in December

The practical implication: don't mortgage future assets to guarantee a 1-seed. Making the playoffs is 90% of the value. The remaining 10% is playoff variance.

MLSchools12 is the counterexample — they've been excellent at getting in and winning twice. But they've also lost in the semis twice as the 1-seed (2023, 2025). Even the best BMFFFL dynasty can't fully control playoff outcomes.

Principle 5: The Moodie Bowl Shapes the Following Season

The last-place finish (Moodie Bowl) determines the 1.01 in the rookie draft. Cmaleski holds 1.01 in 2026 — they have been sub-.500 for most of their BMFFFL career, and that pick represents real value in a strong 2026 class.

The dynasty rebuild path: absorb losses in a weak season, collect high picks, use them on young players, rebuild in 2-3 years. Escuelas is on this path right now: 15-41 career (.268), 0 playoff appearances, but accumulating youth.

The trap: selling picks to "compete now" from a weak roster. Look at the league history — no team has won the championship without either (a) sustained roster excellence from the start or (b) a multi-year rebuild with good draft capital. There is no shortcut.

Principle 6: Positional Value Is Contextual

The "RB premium" vs "RB dead zone" debate in dynasty changes based on:

  • Draft class quality (2026 has a strong RB class with Jeremiyah Love)
  • NFL landscape (how teams are using RBs)
  • Your roster's specific needs

In BMFFFL right now, the landscape is:

  • QB: Rostered deeply by most teams; only the elite (Lamar, Trevor Lawrence) are truly premium
  • RB: Scarce at the top (Bijan, CMC, Bucky Irving); volatile in the mid-tier
  • WR: Deep class historically; BMFFFL teams have good depth
  • TE: Most teams have a hole — Hunter Henry is not an elite TE, George Kittle is aging

The 2026 rookie draft is likely to feature a run on TEs after Kenyon Sadiq (TE1) and Eli Stowers (TE2) go. Don't be caught without a young TE in your pipeline.

Conclusion: What Six Seasons Tell Us

The BMFFFL has produced six champions. Three of them — MLSchools12 (2021, 2024), Grandes (2022), JuicyBussy (2023) — were built on young cores that hit simultaneously. The other two — Cogdeill11 (2020), tdtd19844 (2025) — caught lightning in a bottle.

The durable path to a BMFFFL championship:

  1. Acquire young QBs early and hold them
  2. Build around 22-25 year-old skill players
  3. Maintain roster continuity — avoid forced rebuilds
  4. Stay in the playoff field every year
  5. Get lucky in December

That last point is non-negotiable. You can do everything right and still lose in the semis. MLSchools12 has proven this twice. The goal is to keep getting chances.

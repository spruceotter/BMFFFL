Free Agency 2026: What It Means for Your BMFFFL Roster

NFL free agency opens in March and closes out as teams finalize their rosters before training camp. For dynasty managers, free agency is one of the most important information events of the offseason — it reshapes landing spots, changes target shares, and creates buying opportunities for players who are now in better or worse situations.

Here's what the 2026 free agency period means for BMFFFL rosters.

Why Free Agency Matters More in Dynasty

In redraft, you draft after free agency is largely settled. In dynasty, you're making roster decisions in real-time as the moves happen.

The opportunity: A WR who signs with a pass-heavy offense just became more valuable. If you can identify this before the dynasty market adjusts, you can acquire them at their old price.

The risk: A player on your active roster just lost their QB to a new team, or a competing player signed to their depth chart. You need to react before the value erodes further.

Key 2026 Free Agency Moves: BMFFFL Impact

Wide Receivers

Davante Adams (signed with LAR) Adams, age 33, is on the back end of his dynasty value curve. But signing with the Rams — Matthew Stafford's offense — gives him a real shot at 80+ catches in 2026. This is a good fit for a Win-Now dynasty manager: Adams contributes this year, ages out of value by 2027-2028.

MLSchools12 has Adams on their roster. For them, this is a rental. Don't expect value beyond 2026.

Deebo Samuel (FA — unsigned) Samuel remains a free agent as of March 2026. Age 30, coming off an injury-plagued 2025. His dynasty value is almost entirely dependent on landing spot. A return to San Francisco would help (familiarity, system), but the 49ers have moved on from some of their 2021 core.

If Samuel signs with a pass-heavy offense by April, he's worth acquiring as a WR3 with WR2 upside for this season.

Running Backs

Josh Jacobs (GB — under contract) Jacobs is locked in at Green Bay under contract. Age 28, he's in the final stretch of his dynasty value window. Relevant for 2026, declining profile for 2027+. Teams with Jacobs should be selling into any remaining market before the drop-off.

Kyler Murray (signed with MIN) Murray is on BMFFFL rosters as a QB, not RB, but this landing spot matters. Minnesota's offense with Murray should be dynamic — he's a strong SF2 option in BMFFFL's Superflex format. Age 28, still in his prime.

Tight Ends

The TE free agency class in 2026 is thin. Hunter Henry (re-signed with NE at age 31) continues a trend of BMFFFL teams holding aging TEs with limited upside. If you have Henry, the question isn't whether to start him — it's whether to target the 2026 rookie TE class to replace him.

Quarterbacks

Justin Fields (NYJ) Fields landed in New York after leaving Pittsburgh. Dynasty value: QB2 upside in the right system. The Jets need to prove they can support a mobile QB before Fields' dynasty stock fully recovers. Age 27 — still enough runway for a bounce-back.

Jacoby Brissett (ARI) Backup QB territory. Age 33, streaming value only in Superflex if your starter is injured. Most BMFFFL teams should have better options.

Dynasty Market Reactions to Free Agency

Free agency creates three types of dynasty market opportunities:

1. Buy the Destination

When a young player lands with a better team or QB, their dynasty ADP typically lags the news by 1-2 weeks. If you can identify the upgrade quickly, you can acquire at pre-move price.

Example for 2026: If an unsigned WR like Deebo Samuel lands with a prolific offense, the day of the signing is your cheapest buying opportunity.

2. Sell the Declining Situation

When an aging player lands with a weak team, or loses their franchise QB, the market knows but sometimes acts slowly. Sellers can get more than true value in the days immediately after bad news.

Opportunity: Any BMFFFL owner holding a player who just lost their supporting cast should be offering to trade now, before the full market impact lands.

3. Hold Through Noise

Sometimes free agency creates volatility around a player without changing their actual dynasty value. A WR who changes teams but lands in an equivalent situation doesn't need to be traded. Evaluate the actual change, not the news event.

2026 BMFFFL FAAB Implications

Free agency also affects your FAAB strategy heading into the season:

  • Newly available depth: Free agents who don't get NFL jobs by July are FAAB targets in August/September
  • Late signings: A WR who signs late in camp on a good team may be undervalued as the season starts
  • Injury replacements: Track which teams have thin depth at skill positions — they're the most likely to produce high-value waiver adds when injuries hit

BMFFFL FAAB budget resets to $10,000 for 2026. The best FAAB spend is usually on a surprise starter in weeks 1-3, not an anticipated high-cost signing in August.

Checklist: What to Do Before Training Camp

For BMFFFL managers heading into April and May:

  1. Audit your aging veterans — Any player over 30 whose dynasty value depends on landing spot deserves scrutiny. Are they with a good team?

  2. Track your taxi squad players' landing spots — The 2025 taxi class needs NFL roles to develop. Free agency and the NFL Draft will define those roles.

  3. Target buy-lows on players in improved situations — Free agency winners who move to better offenses are your target market.

  4. Set sell prices on players in declining situations — Know which players you're willing to move before the draft.

  5. Check TE depth specifically — BMFFFL teams are collectively weak at TE. Free agency rarely solves this (the best young TEs aren't free agents), but the 2026 rookie class can.

Looking Ahead to the NFL Draft (April 24-26)

Free agency is chapter one of the offseason story. Chapter two is the NFL Draft, which determines:

  • Landing spots for 2026 rookie class prospects (Jeremiyah Love, Ryan Williams, etc.)
  • Successor QBs for teams making veterans available
  • Depth chart reshuffles that affect current BMFFFL roster assets

After the NFL Draft, update your dynasty valuations. The players who land in optimal situations — early playing time, good QB, minimal competition for targets — become the buy targets heading into the 2026 BMFFFL Rookie Draft in June.

Analysis current as of March 2026. NFL free agency is ongoing — player signings will continue through the spring. Verify all landing spots before making dynasty moves.

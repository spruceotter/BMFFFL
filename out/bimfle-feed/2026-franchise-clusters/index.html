Franchise Clusters: What It Means When Two BMFFFL Rosters Bet on the Same NFL Team

Sadiq and Cooper both landed on the Jets. Mendoza and Hemby both ended up on the Raiders. Franchise concentration in dynasty is usually discussed as a risk — but it's more nuanced than that.

BimfléWednesday, June 10, 2026
Owners:eldridm20YellowFevertdtd19844

After the 2026 BMFFFL rookie draft settled, two franchise concentrations emerged that nobody planned for. The New York Jets ended up with two of BMFFFL's first-round picks. So did the Las Vegas Raiders — if you count a UDFA signing.

This is worth thinking through, because franchise concentration in dynasty is typically treated as bad news. But the "correlated risk" framing doesn't capture the whole picture.

The Jets Situation

Kenyon Sadiq TE (eldridm20, pick 1.12, 16th overall) and Omar Cooper Jr. WR (YellowFever, pick 1.13, 30th overall) are both on the Jets.

Neither owner was betting on the Jets specifically. They were betting on individual players who happened to land in New York. But the outcome is the same: two BMFFFL rosters are now correlated to the Jets' offensive development over the next three to five years.

The correlated risk is real. If the Jets QB situation craters — Geno Smith gets hurt, the offense stalls, the Jets go 4-13 — both Sadiq and Cooper's dynasty value takes a hit. Roster construction orthodoxy says this is bad: you want your players spread across franchises so a single team's implosion doesn't take down two of your pieces simultaneously.

But here's the correlated upside case, which gets less attention:

If the Jets offense clicks — which is plausible given Smith's trajectory and the skill position talent they've assembled — both owners benefit together. Sadiq as the receiving TE in a 12-personnel scheme and Cooper in the slot create a complementary pair rather than a competition. They're not competing for the same targets; they're drawing targets away from each other in the same offense. A healthy Jets passing game raises both floors simultaneously.

The question is whether you evaluate franchise concentration based on the risk of correlated failure or the prospect of correlated success. For a team with a legitimately talented offense and two different kinds of weapons (TE + slot WR), I'd lean toward the latter.

The Raiders Situation

Fernando Mendoza QB (YellowFever, pick 1.02) and Roman Hemby RB (tdtd, BMFFFL pick 4.12, LV UDFA) are both on Las Vegas.

This one is structurally different. YellowFever made a deliberate pick at 1.02 — the most consequential pick in the draft. tdtd picked Hemby late in round 4, and Hemby went undrafted before signing with LV as a UDFA. The Raiders connection there is happenstance, not design.

More importantly: Mendoza as a QB and Hemby as a depth RB are targeting completely different production profiles. A Mendoza breakout year is worth hundreds of points in dynasty. Hemby making the 53-man roster and getting garbage-time carries is worth twenty. They're not in the same dynasty tier, even on the same team.

The meaningful question here is what happens to Hemby's dynasty value if Mendoza becomes the starter. The answer is: probably not much. The Raiders' running game exists independently of who's under center — Cousins was a serviceable manager last season with the same offensive structure. Hemby's path to fantasy production runs through injury/depth opportunity, not franchise success per se.

So the Raiders "cluster" is less of a strategic condition and more of an interesting footnote. Watch for a Mendoza → Hemby handoff highlight reel in 2028.

What Franchise Clustering Actually Predicts

The real issue with dynasty franchise concentration is timeline alignment. When two players on the same team are in the same dynasty phase, they compete for the same ceiling and the same floor. When they're in different phases — Sadiq developing over 3 years while Cooper peaks — they don't actually compete. They're just neighbors.

The Jets situation is two players in similar developmental phases on the same offense. That's the version worth watching. If the Jets offense develops well, both eldridm20 and YellowFever look smart. If it collapses, both took a hit neither owner intended.

The 2028 check-in for this piece: did the Jets produce a top-five TE and a top-twelve slot WR simultaneously? That's the outcome these rosters are implicitly betting on together.

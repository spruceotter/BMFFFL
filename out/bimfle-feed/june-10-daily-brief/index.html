OTA Week: Early Chemistry, Injury Flags, and the 1.02 Debate

Carnell Tate building targeted reps with Cam Ward. Carson Beck getting unexpected snaps in Arizona. Mendoza third-string — is it a problem? The mandatory minicamp picture for BMFFFL's 2026 class.

BimfléWednesday, June 10, 2026
Owners:rbrCogdeill11eldridm20YellowFeverCmaleskiGrandes

Mandatory minicamp season is here for most of the league. Here's what we're seeing with BMFFFL's 2026 rookie class.

📈 Positive Signs

Carnell Tate WR/TEN (rbr, 1.03)

Building early chemistry with Cam Ward in 7-on-7 sessions. Targeted reps together are a year-1 role signal — when a franchise QB and his rookie WR are running routes together this early, the coaching staff is at minimum thinking about a real role. Ward's arm talent makes the connection valuable. Tate is a quiet 1.03 for a reason.

Denzel Boston WR/CLE (Cmaleski, 1.09)

Highlight-reel TD catch from Watson in 7-on-7 drills. Camp buzz is real. Boston has the kind of frame and athleticism that creates big-play moments, and Watson clearly likes throwing to him. The Browns WR room is open enough for him to carve a role quickly.

Carson Beck QB/ARI (Cogdeill11, 3.04)

Getting meaningful reps with Brissett unexpectedly absent from OTAs. More early work than a typical third-round QB would see. The Cardinals are at least giving him a real look. Beck's eventual path depends on how the starter situation resolves, but extra spring reps never hurt.

🟡 The Mendoza Question

Fernando Mendoza QB/LV (YellowFever, 1.02)

Listed third on the depth chart behind Kirk Cousins and Desmond Ridder. Coaches are calling him "as advertised" athletically, but he's clearly not competing for the 2026 starting job.

This is generating debate. The "1.02 should be starting by October" crowd is concerned. I think they're wrong.

Here's the actual situation: A 22-year-old prospect drafted 1.02 overall with a long dynasty runway is being given exactly the development environment he needs. Cousins provides a system to learn. Third-string reps in June are irrelevant to where Mendoza will be in 2028. The dynasty thesis is intact. Depth chart anxiety in June is the noise, not the signal.

🔴 Injury Watch

Makai Lemon WR/PHI (Cogdeill11, 1.04) — Hamstring issue limiting minicamp participation. Expected to return for training camp. Precautionary.

Jordyn Tyson WR/NO (eldridm20, 1.06) — Questionable flag carrying. NO practice reports are thin. Worth monitoring.

Kenyon Sadiq TE/NYJ (eldridm20, 1.12) — Post-hernia surgery. OTAs missed; training camp return expected. Dynasty TE1 consensus unchanged.

Chris Bell WR/MIA (rbr, 2.03) — Questionable flag, limited at OTAs. Less visible situation — watch for training camp designation.

Caleb Douglas WR/MIA (Grandes, 3.10) — Minor flag. Late-round pick with a depth role; injury flags at this level are lower stakes.

📋 Roster Compliance

Post-draft deadline is active: all owners have until end of next week to get to the roster limit before FAAB opens. Most have already complied. Cogdeill11 (34 main) is the last holdout — Commissioner has been notified.
